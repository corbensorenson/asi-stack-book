# Model-adequacy dossier: scalable-oversight refinement

## Decision

Adequate for one finite authored oversight-review lifecycle with exact identity custody, prospective protocol binding, review and outcome-audit gates, typed adjudication, bounded-use handoff, and successor-version readmission. Inadequate for reviewer competence, protocol efficacy, outcome validity, causal interpretation, institutional enforcement, deployed invalidation, or release execution.

## Reachable model

Seven stages bind task and consumer identity plus protocol, cohort, system, supervisor, evidence view, dependencies, baseline, auditor, policy, residual, authority, and protocol version. Fifty-eight routes cover missing scope and permitted-inference boundaries; supervisor/system/capability envelopes; information access and incentives; prospective failure and expiry rules; evidence views, baseline, outcomes, disagreement, denominator, coverage, abstention, and cost; independent outcome audit, auditor separation, dependencies, correlation challenge, outcome criteria, failures; uncertainty, residual ownership, adjudication, competence-inference and policy-authority laundering; bounded-use custody; and stale readmission.

The favorable route is only a bounded-use handoff to a named consumer. A later material change advances the protocol version and returns the record to scoped protocol binding only after descendant invalidation and ordinary-route blocking are recorded. Evidence-backed abstention produces an escalation disposition, not a favorable use judgment.

## Countermodels and consumer

The Lean model proves rejection without state mutation, one-receipt accepted advancement, zero support/effect assignment, eight retained concrete countermodels, and a full bounded-use-to-versioned-readmission witness. The independent Python consumer reruns the inherited seven-case suite, covers all 58 routes, and rejects 65/65 binding, lifecycle, review, audit, abstention, authority, replay, and readmission mutations.

## Assumptions, exclusions, and adequacy verdict

The model trusts every authored identifier, Boolean, protocol record, review outcome, audit result, disagreement, abstention rationale, uncertainty, residual, expiry, invalidation assertion, and authority field. It does not establish their truth, completeness, independence, calibration, causal meaning, effectiveness, or deployed enforcement. Protocol activity is not reviewer competence or outcome truth, and the lifecycle assigns no support state. Support-state effect: exactly `none`.
