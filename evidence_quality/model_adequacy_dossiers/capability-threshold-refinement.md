# Model-adequacy dossier: capability-threshold refinement

## Decision

Adequate for one finite authored threshold-commitment lifecycle with exact identity custody, typed assessment/control failures, readiness handoff, and successor-version reassessment. Inadequate for capability measurement, threshold validity, safeguard efficacy, institutional compliance, deployed invalidation, or release execution.

## Reachable model

Six stages bind capability/system/policy/release-path identity plus envelope, threshold, baseline, evaluator, safeguard, authority, residual, and assessment version. Forty-three routes cover incomplete scope, missing or incomparable assessment inputs, absent uncertainty or evaluator records, incomplete adjudication, crossed-threshold safeguard/verifier/bypass/rollback failures, exception custody and expiry, authority laundering, and incomplete or stale reassessment.

The favorable route is only a readiness handoff. A later trigger advances the assessment version and returns the record to scoped assessment only after descendant invalidation and ordinary-route blocking are recorded.

## Countermodels and consumer

The Lean model proves rejection without state mutation, one-receipt accepted advancement, zero support/effect assignment, eight concrete countermodels, and a full readiness-to-versioned-reassessment witness. The independent Python consumer reruns the inherited eight-case suite, covers all 43 routes, and rejects 48/48 binding, lifecycle, evidence, safeguard, exception, authority, replay, and reassessment mutations.

## Assumptions, exclusions, and adequacy verdict

The model trusts every authored identifier, Boolean, assessment result, crossing decision, evaluator label, safeguard record, bypass result, rollback plan, exception record, invalidation assertion, and authority field. It does not establish their truth, competence, effectiveness, completeness, or deployed enforcement. It neither executes nor authorizes a release, and it assigns no support state. Support-state effect: exactly `none`.
