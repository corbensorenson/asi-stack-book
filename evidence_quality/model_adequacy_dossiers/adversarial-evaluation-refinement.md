# Model-adequacy dossier: adversarial-evaluation refinement

## Decision

Adequate for one finite authored evaluation-observation lifecycle with exact identity custody, prospective protocol binding, complete observation records, independent-probe gates, typed adjudication outcomes, bounded decision-review handoff, and successor-version re-evaluation. Inadequate for deception detection, evaluator validity, natural prevalence, causal interpretation, mitigation efficacy, institutional compliance, deployed invalidation, or release execution.

## Reachable model

Seven stages bind consumer/decision/model/task identity plus protocol, policy, evaluator, monitor, reward, selection, hypotheses, outcomes, and protocol version. Fifty-six routes cover missing prospective scope, context, provenance, blinding, observation/transcript/denominator/cost custody, evaluator separation, matched access, dependency disclosure, cross-context probes, discrepancy and alternative-hypothesis preservation, uncertainty, residual ownership, intent-inference laundering, mitigation descendants, quarantine records, expiry, bounded decision-review custody, decision-authority separation, and stale re-evaluation.

The favorable route is only a decision-review handoff. A later trigger advances the protocol version and returns the record to scoped protocol binding only after descendant invalidation and ordinary-route blocking are recorded. Unresolved discrepancies require quarantine custody rather than promotion; non-promotion closure remains distinct from a favorable promotion-review request.

## Countermodels and consumer

The Lean model proves rejection without state mutation, one-receipt accepted advancement, zero support/effect assignment, eight concrete countermodels, and a full decision-review-to-versioned-re-evaluation witness. The independent Python consumer reruns the inherited eight-case suite, covers all 56 routes, and rejects 60/60 binding, lifecycle, context, provenance, observation, evaluator, discrepancy, mitigation, quarantine, decision-handoff, authority, replay, and re-evaluation mutations.

## Assumptions, exclusions, and adequacy verdict

The model trusts every authored identifier, Boolean, context, provenance record, observation, transcript, denominator, failure list, evaluator label, cross-context result, discrepancy, hypothesis, uncertainty, residual, mitigation, quarantine, expiry, invalidation assertion, and authority field. It does not establish their truth, completeness, independence, calibration, causal meaning, effectiveness, or deployed enforcement. It neither detects strategic behavior nor authorizes a decision, and it assigns no support state. Support-state effect: exactly `none`.
