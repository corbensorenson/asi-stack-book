## Core obligation

Show that a typed Cognitive Kernel ABI can host genuinely heterogeneous learned
or exact kernels for an exact route envelope without losing required capability,
exact state, authority ceilings, evidence lineage, fallback, rollback, or honest
total-cost accounting.

## Owned subordinate obligations

- proposal/effect and authority separation;
- exact/latent state separation and checkpoint compatibility;
- architecture-neutral reflex, reaction, deliberation, and specialist routes;
- fair Transformer, SSM/recurrent, convolutional, test-time-state, external
  memory, KAN, continuous-time, program-learning, and hybrid comparisons;
- total-system KISS accounting;
- the falsifiable OneCell/frozen-core ratchet;
- governed architectural RSI with independent evaluation and recovery.

## Promotion path

Implement three heterogeneous kernels behind one independently checked ABI;
reject malicious kernels; prove and refine the finite interface invariants; run
matched equal-active-parameter and equal-total-lifecycle-cost tournaments on
natural and adversarial workloads; reproduce and transfer any route-level win;
then adjudicate atoms individually. An architecture name, source-reported result,
or green interface validator cannot promote the core claim.

## Defeat conditions

The ABI loses necessary competitive capability, exact-state migration is not
possible, adapter/routing/governance burden dominates, online state cannot be
attributed or rolled back, architecture-family gains vanish under matched total
cost, the OneCell ratchet hides external intelligence or fails held-out transfer,
or a strong simple baseline dominates the complete measured frontier.

## Current evidence

Twenty-three source records have bounded mappings and notes: two author-intent
conversations, three local architecture/governance sources, and eighteen primary
or critical external comparators. No architecture was implemented, reproduced,
qualified, or promoted locally.
