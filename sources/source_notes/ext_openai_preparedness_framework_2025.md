## Thesis

The framework presents an organization-specific process for tracking selected
frontier capabilities, mapping threshold levels to operational commitments, and
using capabilities and safeguards reports in deployment decisions. It provides
a comparator for separating a measured capability, a threshold decision, a
safeguard-verification record, residual-risk review, and a release authority.
It does not establish that any threshold taxonomy is complete or that reported
safeguards sufficiently minimize risk in another system.

## Mechanisms

- Select capability categories through stated risk criteria and distinguish
  tracked from research categories.
- Map high and critical capability thresholds to distinct deployment and
  development safeguard requirements.
- Separate capability reports from safeguards reports and record residual-risk
  review and organizational recommendations before a deployment decision.
- Reassess safeguards when new evidence or a changed frontier-risk landscape is
  identified instead of treating a threshold outcome as permanent clearance.

## Evidence

- The official page describes the framework's own categories, threshold levels,
  report types, review process, and stated reassessment commitments.
- It is a policy and process description, not an independent measurement of
  capability, safeguard efficacy, or deployment safety.
- This repository has not run a preparedness evaluation, categorized a model,
  verified a safeguard, conducted a residual-risk review, or made a deployment
  decision. The source is a policy-structure comparator only.

## Failure Modes

- Capability categories can omit a relevant risk or blur domain-specific
  differences under one aggregate threshold.
- A threshold outcome can be treated as release clearance without a distinct
  safeguard-efficacy, residual-risk, and authority review.
- A policy exception or changed competitive landscape can weaken a commitment
  unless the rationale, approver, scope, and residuals are durable.
- Automated evaluations can outrun the depth or scope of the review required
  for a high-consequence deployment decision.

## Claims To Add Or Update

- Use this note for separate capability, safeguard, residual-risk, and release
  records; threat-category scope; threshold-linked commitments; and reassessment
  conditions.
- Require a threshold result to state what it can change and what separate
  authority must still decide.
- Do not claim a local capability category, threshold crossing, safeguard
  efficacy, deployment safety, policy compliance, or ASI.

## Open Questions

- How should one threshold record preserve non-equivalence among cyber,
  biological, autonomy, and other capability domains?
- Which reassessment triggers are strong enough to reopen a prior non-crossing
  decision without allowing arbitrary policy drift?
- How can a public-safe fixture prove that an uncrossed or crossed threshold
  still cannot bypass readiness, approval, or residual-escrow gates?
