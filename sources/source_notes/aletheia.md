## Thesis

Aletheia frames advanced AI as truth manufacturing rather than fluent generation. It introduces an epistemic engine that acquires context, decomposes claims, verifies against evidence, routes uncertainty, and applies constitutional governance before outputs or actions become trusted.

## Mechanisms

- Use active context acquisition before hypothesis formation.
- Maintain layered semantic representation from symbols through primitives, propositions, claims, and dossiers.
- Route work by risk, uncertainty, ambiguity, and historical failure.
- Use a tribunal or verification gate to test claims against contracts, logic, evidence, and live oracles.
- Preserve unknowns as explicit aporea rather than guessing.
- Use claim-native artifacts, assurance levels, source-bounded review, replay, adversarial tests, and incident observability.
- Apply export boundaries, template governance, schema evolution, and change-control procedures.

## Evidence

- The source is a whitepaper and technical specification with multiple variants in the raw cache.
- It provides strong architecture material for evidence states, claim ledgers, verification gates, and governed outputs.
- It does not provide local implementation, live-oracle runs, adversarial test results, or proof artifacts in this repository.

## Failure Modes

- Treating verification theater as truth manufacturing.
- Fetching current context without source-quality or recency discipline.
- Letting tribunal agents share correlated blind spots.
- Overrouting every claim into expensive review and causing latency collapse.
- Failing to preserve unknowns, contradictions, and rejected claims.
- Giving the verifier execution authority instead of evidence authority.
