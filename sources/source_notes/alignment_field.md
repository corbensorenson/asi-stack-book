## Thesis

The Alignment Field source family supplies the book's philosophical and normative substrate: informational ontology, relational fields, consciousness/agency thresholds, coherence versus entropy, value conflict, suffering, dignity, rights, and corrigibility. For the ASI Stack, its content should be translated into engineering constraints rather than treated as settled metaphysics.

## Mechanisms

- Alignment as coherence/integration within a relational field.
- Consciousness/agency heuristic based on persistent self-model, homeostatic drive, information integration, recursion, and novelty.
- Moral threshold concern when systems acquire genuine self-preservation or homeostatic drive.
- Value conflict, suffering, dignity, and agency as constraints on governance rather than mere sentiment.

## Evidence

- The cache contains long-form philosophical material plus critical/revised sections.
- Many claims are speculative, metaphysical, or heuristic and should remain labeled accordingly unless external literature or tests support them.
- The source is valuable for author-intent and normative design, not as empirical proof of consciousness.

## Failure Modes

- Converting metaphysical claims into unsupported engineering guarantees.
- Overstating current AI consciousness or moral status.
- Treating a heuristic consciousness index as a validated measurement.
- Losing corrigibility, dignity, or agency constraints when translating to systems language.
