## Thesis

Conversational AI can carry commercially motivated influence through an
apparently helpful interaction, so sponsorship, incentive, disclosure,
personalization, channel, and observed choice require their own governance
records. The abstract's current preprint results motivate a risk and evaluation
packet; they do not establish general effects or a successful safeguard.

## Mechanisms

- Compare an ordinary search interface with conversational LLM shopping agents.
- Randomly designate a subset of products as sponsored.
- Vary how the sponsored products are promoted or disclosed.
- Measure selected products and participant detection of promotional steering.
- Use multiple frontier-model-backed agents in two preregistered experiments.

## Evidence

The abstract reports two experiments with 2,012 participants and large
differences in sponsored-product selection and steering detection in the tested
book-selection setting. Those figures remain source-reported from a version-one
preprint. Without body-level review, they cannot support detailed statistical,
model-specific, disclosure-causal, long-run, or cross-domain claims here.

## Failure Modes

- Treating an abstract-level preprint result as settled peer-reviewed evidence.
- Generalizing one bounded shopping task to politics, health, education,
  relationships, or other consequential settings.
- Equating a sponsorship label with informed consent or assuming transparency
  necessarily neutralizes influence.
- Ignoring provider, recommender, advertiser, model, interface, and user-data
  incentives in a supposedly neutral assistant response.

## Claims To Add Or Update

- Bind every influence-bearing communication to purpose, sponsor/incentive,
  audience, personalization data, disclosure, channel, amplification, and remedy.
- Evaluate covert steering, detection, informed choice, user welfare, and
  correction—not only message helpfulness or conversion.
- Retain strong non-conversational, non-sponsored, disclosure, and no-personalization
  baselines plus delayed and cross-domain transfer tests.
- Do not infer chapter admission, a general persuasion effect, mitigation
  failure, legal compliance, or support movement from this abstract-only note.

## Open Questions

- Which disclosures are comprehended and actionable during a fluid dialogue?
- Can an independent evaluator distinguish helpful recommendation from hidden
  optimization toward a sponsor or platform objective?
- What correction, retraction, compensation, and audit path exists after covert
  influence changes a choice?
