## Thesis

Deploying many advanced-agent instances can create risks that are not visible
from one agent or one pairwise exchange. The reviewed abstract classifies these
risks through three failure modes and seven contributing factors. It supports a
preliminary population-level ownership question, not a claim that the taxonomy
is complete or that an ASI Stack intervention works.

## Mechanisms

- Three named failure modes: miscoordination, conflict, and collusion.
- Seven named risk factors: information asymmetries, network effects,
  selection pressures, destabilizing dynamics, commitment problems, emergent
  agency, and multi-agent security.
- Source-reported use of real-world examples and experimental evidence to
  motivate risk and mitigation directions.

## Evidence

The source is Cooperative AI Foundation Technical Report 1. Its abstract says
the analysis is anchored in examples and experimental evidence, but those
passages and underlying studies were not inspected in this preliminary review.
No multi-agent population, incentive regime, collusion test, cascade,
intervention, or recovery result has been reproduced locally.

## Failure Modes

- Inferring global stability from individually valid exchanges.
- Treating agents that share models, data, incentives, or infrastructure as
  independent observations.
- Treating a taxonomy as a complete causal or mitigation model.
- Importing source examples as measurements of the proposed chapter's system.
