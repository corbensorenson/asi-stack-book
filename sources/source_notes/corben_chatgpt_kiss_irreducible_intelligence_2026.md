## Thesis

The simplest useful cognitive architecture may be a small recurring proposal
kernel surrounded by exact state, branch-preserving search, verification, and a
ratchet that compiles repeated verified solutions into reusable abstractions.

## Mechanisms

- Search, verify, then compile repeated successes.
- Separate exact identity/effect state from latent heuristic state.
- Measure simplicity over the entire system, including hidden search and tools.
- Freeze the core while testing whether verified skill accumulation lowers future search.
- Treat the learned model as a replaceable heuristic accelerator rather than the
  database, authority, verifier, planner, and world model at once.
- Route evidence acquisition by value of computation across memory, tools,
  clarification, experiments, and active perception.
- Preserve the central ratchet: expensive search becomes independently verified,
  executable, reusable structure that should reduce future search.

## Evidence

This is a design conversation supplied by Corben. It records desired architecture

## Failure Modes

- A tiny core can hide a large adapter, corpus, search budget, or verifier.
- Verified compression can merely rename benchmark-specific traces.
- A fixed core may plateau or depend on privileged retrieval.
- A short description of the kernel can hide unbounded search, memory,
  human-authored primitives, or verification cost; complexity is relocated, not erased.
