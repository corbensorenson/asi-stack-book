## Thesis

Open Collective is relevant to Artifact Steward Agents because it provides an existing pattern for transparent community money management and fiscal hosting. It can inform steward treasury records, public ledgers, expense review, and legal-entity delegation, but it does not prove autonomous treasury safety or make an AI steward legally authorized to spend.

## Mechanisms

- Let communities collect, manage, and disburse money with public transparency.
- Use fiscal hosts to hold funds, handle accounting, taxes, and administrative obligations.
- Support contribution intake, expenses, budgets, and public financial records.
- Separate collective project activity from the legal entity that holds funds.
- Provide a near-term reference for proposal-first project funding workflows.

## Evidence

- The official documentation describes transparent collection, management, and disbursement of community money and explains the role of fiscal hosts.
- This repository has not created a collective, selected a fiscal host, processed expenses, linked a treasury policy, or audited a legal workflow.
- Use this source for funding substrate vocabulary and fiscal-hosting constraints only.

## Failure Modes

- Transparent finances do not by themselves decide whether spending is valuable, lawful, mission-aligned, or evidence-sufficient.
- Fiscal hosting introduces legal, tax, eligibility, fee, and host-mission constraints that a steward must not bypass.
- A public ledger can expose sensitive contributor, donor, or operational information if policy boundaries are weak.
- AI-generated spending proposals still require human, maintainer, host, or governance review.
