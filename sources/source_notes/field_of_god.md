## Thesis

The Field of God is a metaphysical and alignment-lineage corpus centered on consciousness, coherence, value, and the alignment field. For this technical book, it should be used as author-intent and philosophical lineage for alignment vocabulary, not as empirical evidence.

## Mechanisms

- Organize the alignment worldview into ontology, consciousness functionals, alignment dynamics, coherence attractors, ethics as geometry, AI futures, genealogy, and conclusion.
- Treat alignment as relational coherence rather than only instruction following.
- Connect consciousness, agency, moral salience, and AI governance through field-like metaphors.
- Emphasize that powerful AI should increase coherence without destroying plurality, agency, or dignity.

## Evidence

- The source is philosophical/metaphysical and contains multiple drafts or title explorations.
- It can inform vocabulary, framing, and authorial intent for the alignment chapters.
- It should not be cited as technical evidence for consciousness, physics, morality, or AI safety claims without additional external or formal support.

## Failure Modes

- Letting metaphysical language make operational claims vague.
- Treating coherence as automatically good without conflict, consent, or pluralism.
- Overstating consciousness claims for AI systems.
- Conflating spiritual framing with evidence for mechanisms.
- Making the alignment chapter less testable by importing too much speculative ontology.
