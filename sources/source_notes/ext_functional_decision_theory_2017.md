## Thesis

Functional decision theory evaluates which output of a decision function would
lead to the best outcome and differs from causal and evidential decision
theories on classic problems. It is a normative proposal, not settled policy.

## Mechanisms

- Treat the decision as the output of a mathematical function.
- Compare counterpossible consequences of alternative outputs.
- Contrast recommendations with CDT and EDT.

## Evidence

The paper provides philosophical and formal motivation across selected
decision problems. It does not establish a universally correct rule, empirical
performance, or safe multi-agent behavior.

## Failure Modes

- A decision theory silently embedded in a planner.
- Logical dependence assumptions mismatched to the environment.
- One-shot puzzle performance generalized to institutions.
- Agent copies coordinating in ways that harm outsiders.
