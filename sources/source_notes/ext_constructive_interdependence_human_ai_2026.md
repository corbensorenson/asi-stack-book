## Thesis

Team reward does not reveal how participants contributed or depended on one
another. Constructive interdependence is a useful candidate measure for
distinguishing genuine cooperation from parallel or substitutive performance.

## Mechanisms

- Represent action interactions in a STRIPS formalism.
- Measure reliance among human and agent actions.
- Compare task reward with teaming and interdependence outcomes.

## Evidence

The authors report low interdependence for some high-performing studied teams.
The result is bounded to the domain, participants, agents, metrics, and study.
It does not establish a universal cooperation metric.

## Failure Modes

- High task reward laundered into a teamwork claim.
- A human reduced to monitoring or cleanup while the agent receives credit.
- Interdependence optimized as an end rather than a task-relative diagnostic.
- Learned human models substituted for actual people without separation.
