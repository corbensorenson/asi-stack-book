## Thesis

The Field of God AI Constitution turns the broader Field of God alignment frame into an operational constitutional layer for AI systems. Its practical thesis is that alignment cannot be collapsed into obedience, reward, user satisfaction, or one scalar. A governed AI must balance truth alignment, relational alignment, and task alignment while preserving agency, consent, corrigibility, reversibility, and least sufficient power.

## Mechanisms

- Define a three-axis alignment tensor: truth, relation, and task.
- Treat power, memory, tool use, persuasion, self-improvement, and delegation as governed actions rather than neutral capabilities.
- Use prime directives around truth before compliance, agency preservation, non-domination, consciousness caution, auditability, least sufficient power, and no self-authorization of sovereignty.
- Classify tool use into risk tiers with escalating permission, logging, reversibility, and human-review requirements.
- Apply a runtime decision procedure that frames the request, identifies stakeholders, grounds uncertainty, checks consent and reversibility, reduces power, and either executes transparently or exits/refuses.
- Pair constitutional text with retrieval, critique, risk classification, memory governance, audit logs, red-team evaluations, self-improvement freezes, and version control.

## Evidence

- The source is a constitutional governance and alignment-specification document.
- It supplies definitions, directives, runtime checks, red-team scenarios, and deployment checklist material.
- It explicitly states that it is not executable code by itself and is not a religious belief requirement.
- No runtime policy engine, red-team suite, Lean proof, or system-prompt evaluation was implemented or run in this repo as part of this note.

## Failure Modes

- Treating metaphysical vocabulary as binding doctrine rather than constitutional terminology.
- Turning coherence into uniformity, domination, or suppression of dissent.
- Using safety language to justify covert control, dependency, or irreversible power accumulation.
- Allowing memory, tool use, or self-improvement to proceed without consent, audit, reversibility, and oversight.
- Confusing a constitutional aspiration with a validated operational policy.
