## Thesis

Within a model/data regime, language-model cross-entropy can follow empirical
power-law trends with model size, data, and compute. Such fits are planning
tools, not universal laws or direct capability forecasts.

## Mechanisms

- Fit loss against parameter, data, and compute scale.
- Analyze overfitting and compute allocation.
- Extrapolate only within a stated family and regime.

## Evidence

The results are source-reported. No ASI Stack model, dataset, fit, or forecast
has been reproduced.

## Failure Modes

- Extrapolating beyond architecture, data, optimizer, or metric regime.
- Converting loss prediction into a downstream capability or safety forecast.
- Ignoring uncertainty, fit breakpoints, and failed runs.
- Treating scale as the only route to efficiency.
