## Thesis

Long-lived AI needs **semantic continuity**: the ability to preserve and replay
what its terms meant across changes to models, prompts, embeddings, tools,
ontologies, evidence, and policy. The Platonic World Model (PWM) proposes a
governed semantic substrate whose stable Form families, immutable Form versions,
typed Essence Contracts, contexts, world branches, grounding adapters,
attestations, commitments, proofs, and semantic transactions form a semantic
constitution around replaceable neural models.

“Platonic” is operational, not a claim that every concept has one timeless
essence. Stable reference and governed revision are the goal. Multiple theories,
jurisdictions, granularities, and contested concepts may coexist without being
silently merged.

## Core Object Distinctions

- **Form family versus Form version.** The family is a stable lineage reference;
  the version is immutable and sufficient for exact historical replay.
- **Lexeme versus concept.** Surface strings are many-to-many interfaces and
  never establish identity or authority by equality alone.
- **Essence Contract versus recognizer.** A typed contract declares the selected
  conceptual commitments; a neural or procedural recognizer is a qualified
  witness, not the definition.
- **Particular, situation, event, and branch.** Instances and time-indexed world
  state remain separate from concepts and from planned, simulated, fictional,
  or counterfactual worlds.
- **Proposition, attestation, commitment, and proof.** Content, source events,
  governed stance, and replayable justification are different objects.
- **Descriptive classification versus operational authority.** A semantic edit
  may change who fits a description but cannot grant access or power without a
  separately authorized policy transition.

## Mechanisms

The proposed mechanism is a six-plane semantic substrate joined by immutable
Form versions, typed Essence Contracts, explicit world branches, qualified
grounding adapters, evidence-bearing commitments, and governed semantic
transactions. Stable identities and versioned contracts preserve replay;
branch, context, and authority gates prevent predictions or descriptive edits
from silently changing actuality or permission; dependency-aware diffs and
migrations make self-editing reviewable. The six planes below separate these
responsibilities so a well-typed concept, a supported proposition, and an
authorized action cannot be collapsed into one model score.

## Six-Plane Architecture

1. **Form Plane:** stable concepts, relations, roles, qualities, actions, norms,
   theories, contexts, and inference profiles.
2. **Particular and Situation Plane:** entities, observations, states, events,
   histories, and beliefs in explicitly named worlds.
3. **Dynamics and Modal Plane:** action schemas, causal models, transition
   models, plans, simulations, counterfactuals, and branch promotion rules.
4. **Grounding Plane:** sensors, measurements, databases, proof procedures,
   simulators, skills, human adjudication, and prototype geometry.
5. **Epistemic Plane:** sources, attestations, commitments, proofs, uncertainty,
   contradictions, defeaters, and open questions.
6. **Semantic Governance Plane:** immutable versions, diffs, dependencies,
   migrations, authorization, quarantine, rollback, conflict resolution, and
   federation trust.

The planes constrain one another but should not collapse. A well-typed claim can
remain unsupported; a high-confidence claim can remain unauthorized; a predicted
state can remain outside actuality.

## Typed Essence Contracts

An Essence Contract records ontological kind, lexical interfaces, constitutive
constraints, diagnostics, prototypes, exclusions, identity and persistence,
relations, affordances, permitted dynamics, grounding, epistemic state, and
governance metadata. Clauses are typed as constitutive, diagnostic,
prototypical, operational, causal/functional, normative, or
implementation-specific. Their strength, context, exceptions, evidence, and
defeaters remain explicit.

Different kinds require different profiles. Mathematical Forms emphasize axioms
and proof; natural kinds emphasize mechanisms, lineage, variation, and
intervention; artifacts include function, construction, repair, and failure;
roles include conferral, rights, temporal validity, and revocation; events use
participants and temporal structure; qualities use units and measurement;
norms require authority and jurisdiction; representations distinguish token,
content, fidelity, and provenance. Identity, membership, recognition confidence,
typicality, and task qualification are deliberately separate judgments.

## Grounding and World Dynamics

- Grounding contracts declare input domain, implementation, output evidence,
  calibration, qualified contexts, known failures, confidence semantics, and
  recency.
- Independent modalities may triangulate evidence, but correlated models do not
  count as independent witnesses merely because their outputs agree.
- Active interventions and counterfactual perturbations test whether proposed
  concepts carry causal or functional structure rather than shortcuts.
- Action Forms declare roles, preconditions, effects, explicit non-effects,
  authority, observability, reversibility, cost, safety constraints, and failure
  modes.
- Transition models are plural and qualified by context, state vocabulary,
  intervention semantics, evidence, cost, and conflict policy.
- Predicted, planned, simulated, fictional, and counterfactual states remain
  branch-local. Actuality changes only through an authorized execution,
  observation, or adoption event.

## Epistemic and Temporal Contract

Every consequential proposition is interpreted under an explicit context and
semantic-basis manifest. Valid time, observation time, and transaction time are
separate. Contexts include world branch, assumptions, jurisdiction, perspective,
theory, granularity, authority, and logic profile; claims cross contexts only
through explicit bridges.

The proposed commitment ladder distinguishes mentioned, attested, corroborated,
validated, verified, and operationally authorized states. These are typed
statuses, not one confidence scalar. Proof objects preserve premises,
attestations, inference versions, assumptions, semantic basis, tools, open
defeaters, signatures, and replay dependencies. Historical replayability,
validity under the original basis, validity under the current preferred basis,
and present operational authorization remain separate statuses.

## Governed Semantic Self-Editing

A generative model proposes a typed semantic transaction; it does not ordinarily
write canonical meaning directly. The transaction passes schema validation,
collision checks, competency questions, adversarial examples, grounding tests,
semantic diff, dependency and blast-radius analysis, authority checks, affected
proof/plan/policy replay, quarantine or shadow use, independent approval where
risk requires it, immutable commit, migration, monitoring, and rollback or
containment.

Semantic diffs separately report contract, extension, entailment, grounding,
dynamics, normative, mapping, and operational deltas. Changes are classified as
documentation, compatible extension, behavioral revision, breaking revision,
fork, deprecation, or revocation. Every dependent artifact receives an explicit
migration disposition. Concurrent changes name an immutable parent and use
semantic conflict analysis rather than text merging.

Experience may update particulars and propose hypotheses, but canonical Forms
change only through the governed upward path. Rollback records semantic, data,
model/tool, compensating external, and irreversible dimensions separately.

## Search–Verify–Compile and Runtime Packets

Concept induction is triggered by persistent residuals, stable unexplained
clusters, reusable plan fragments, causal variables, contradiction-resolving
distinctions, compression gains, transfer failures, new institutions, or trusted
teaching. Candidates carry positive, negative, and boundary cases; a provisional
contract; prototype geometry; grounding; utility; alternatives; and defeaters.
They are tested for compression, prediction, control, transfer, discrimination,
groundability, stability, compositionality, explanation, and governance cost.

Accepted concepts mature from ephemeral hypothesis through research candidate,
provisional, qualified, canonical, deprecated, or forked Forms. Compilation
produces stable identity, contracts, schemas, recognizers, inference and
transition interfaces, proof obligations, competency questions, and runtime
packet templates.

The packet compiler selects the smallest sufficient, version-pinned semantic
neighborhood for a task after mandatory safety, authority, ambiguity, and
defeater dependencies are satisfied. A router then chooses lookup, graph,
temporal, causal, formal, probabilistic, or language-model reasoning. Cache
freshness depends on semantic versions, grounder calibration, world state,
defeaters, authority, mappings, and validity intervals. Task residue preserves
concepts used, new entities, commitments, proof handles, ambiguities, proposals,
and packet dependencies.

## Federation and Trusted Kernel

Agents exchange signed semantic packets and typed mappings—exact, contextual,
broader, narrower, overlapping, translated, analogous, successor, disputed, or
incompatible—without forcing a global merge. Interoperability can be task-local,
directional, expiring, and explicit about information loss. External packets do
not write local canonical semantics automatically.

Self-description bottoms out in a minimized trusted semantic kernel that defines
content identity, append-only events, signatures, branch separation, version
resolution, and constitutional authorization. Kernel changes require dual
execution, invariant replay, external authorization, and preserved recovery.

## Constitutional Invariants

- Committed history is immutable and exact versions remain resolvable.
- No inference silently substitutes a different semantic version.
- Contexts and world branches do not leak without explicit bridges.
- Predictions and simulations cannot directly modify actuality.
- Semantic changes cannot grant authority by implication.
- Unknown is not false and unsupported is not disproved.
- Grounding regimes and implementation qualifications are declared.
- Canonical commitments retain provenance and semantic basis.
- Breaking changes expose dependencies and migration dispositions.
- Contradictions remain local rather than enabling arbitrary inference.
- Generators propose; authorized governance commits.
- Replacing a recognizer or simulator cannot silently rewrite a Form.
- Mapping equivalence is scoped and governed.
- Required unresolved dependencies cause explicit degradation or fail-closed
  behavior.
- Packet compilation and federation obey purpose-bound information flow.

## Failure Modes

- Ontology poisoning, lexical alias injection, and equivalence abuse.
- Evidence laundering and context or modality confusion.
- Semantic downgrade, replay, grounding spoofing, and branch escape.
- Authority escalation through descriptive reclassification.
- Dependency-index evasion and incomplete blast-radius analysis.
- Concept explosion, premature reification, and maintenance decay.
- Sensitive semantic-neighborhood exfiltration through packets or federation.
- Governance capture, ideological ossification, and false essentialism.
- Intractable cross-profile reasoning and human-unreadable diffs.
- A precise substrate that costs more than the failures it prevents.

## Evidence and Falsification Program

The proposed evaluation compares a full PWM against retrieval-only LLMs,
vector-memory agents, conventional property/RDF graphs, OWL/SHACL with
provenance, context-aware graphs, latent/object-centric world models, and
concept-bottleneck or embedding systems. Ablations remove immutable bases,
claim separation, context bridges, anti-examples, authority non-escalation,
dependency analysis, and branch protection.

Longitudinal suites should induce semantic drift, context/modality confusion,
multimodal boundary cases, long-horizon planning with contradictory evidence,
model replacement, and federated mapping disputes. Measure silent equivocation,
basis completeness, historical replay, migration precision/recall, context
leakage, branch contamination, defeater recall, unsupported commitment,
anti-example robustness, shortcut sensitivity, planning success, safety
violations, patch rejection, blast-radius recall, rollback, latency, storage,
review burden, and ontology maintenance cost.

The architecture is weakened where well-tuned simpler baselines achieve equal
continuity and safety at lower total cost; Essence Contracts do not improve
boundary behavior; dependency analysis misses consequential effects; packet
compilation dominates execution; context machinery merely relocates errors;
concept induction proliferates unstable Forms; or adapters reinterpret shared
concepts incompatibly.

## Claims To Add Or Update

- Treat semantic continuity as an explicit systems property across model,
  ontology, grounding, evidence, and policy replacement.
- Require every consequential conclusion to name its semantic basis, context,
  world branch, support path, grounding implementations, and migration impact.
- Separate semantic well-formedness, epistemic support, and operational
  authority.
- Treat recognizers as qualified witnesses, not definitions.
- Protect the actual-world ledger from predicted and simulated branch writes.
- Make semantic self-editing a transaction with typed diffs, blast radius,
  migrations, authority, quarantine, and rollback/irreversibility accounting.
- Use open-world, plural, task-local mappings instead of forced global
  equivalence.
