## Thesis

Talos treats AI work as a labor operating system: typed jobs, deterministic control planes, evidence, isolation, audit, replay, and delivery replace informal agent activity. It supplies the execution/labor layer for the ASI Stack.

## Mechanisms

- Typed job lifecycle with decomposition, contract lock, factory execution, adjudication, delivery, and feedback/autopoiesis.
- Deterministic cognitive manufacturing, intelligence arbitrage, epistemic certainty, sovereign isolation, and zero-copy logistics.
- Source allow-listing, blind secret handles, Digital SCIFs, evidence records, audit logs, and replay.
- Work promotion through tests, residuals, regression coverage, and controlled runtime adapters.

## Evidence

- The local cache contains many Talos versions and public-release sections.
- It is useful as an execution-OS design source, not as proof that any benchmark result has been reproduced in this repo.
- Security and benchmark claims require separate artifacts before support-state promotion.

## Failure Modes

- Tool calls bypassing contract locks or human approval.
- Secret material entering model context instead of staying behind handles.
- Missing provenance, non-replayable jobs, unverifiable evidence, and forgotten failures.
- Overstating benchmark or security claims without test records.
