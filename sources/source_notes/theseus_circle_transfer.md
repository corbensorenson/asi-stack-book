## Thesis

The Theseus Circle Calculus transfer lane is a report-only bridge from Circle finite fixtures into private Theseus benchmark design. Its core claim is intentionally narrow: Circle Calculus can supply deterministic fixtures and contract families that configure private experiments. It is not model-quality, promotion, transfer, or ASI evidence by itself.

## Mechanisms

- Consume Circle AI contract packs into `circle_ai_contract_consumer` reports.
- Define private workload-smoke and proxy-benchmark layers for recurrence budgets, candidate coverage, memory alias visibility, phase-feature invariance, mixer parameter accounting, and seed-rule regeneration.
- Separate quality, runtime, memory, parameter count, interpretability, transfer, and failure-case axes for every contract.
- Mark most claim axes as not measured or planned private eval until a named workload, ordinary baseline, negative control, metric, script, and report exist.
- Prohibit exporting private Theseus data back into Circle through this bridge.

## Evidence

- The source is a local integration note.
- It names scripts, reports, contract families, structural smoke workloads, deterministic proxy benchmark names, allowed claims, and disallowed claims.
- The source explicitly states that the consumer does not call inference, mutate training data, write promotion evidence, or prove Theseus capability gains.
- No Circle-to-Theseus consumer, smoke workload, or proxy benchmark was executed from this repo as part of this note.

## Failure Modes

- Treating Circle fixtures as evidence of model quality or ASI progress.
- Mixing deterministic structural smoke checks with learned-model benchmark claims.
- Failing to attach ordinary baselines, negative controls, metrics, scripts, and reports before quality/runtime/memory claims.
- Leaking private Theseus data back into public Circle artifacts.
