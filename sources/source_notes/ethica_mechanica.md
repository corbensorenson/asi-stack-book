## Thesis

Ethica Mechanica is a philosophical and socio-technical treatise that frames reality, mind, agency, and constitutional systems through emergent information. Its book value is mainly as alignment and governance lineage: AI governance should preserve contestability, agency, and recursive public correction rather than freeze one authority layer.

## Mechanisms

- Treat mind as a cybernetic mechanism for navigation and error correction.
- Define recursive agency as a system's ability to model itself, act, receive feedback, and revise.
- Translate metaphysical claims into governance design through the idea of a recursive republic or contestable library.
- Assign AI a logistics role in managing argument, evidence, and institutional memory rather than sovereign moral authority.
- Stress-test the system through objections about emergence, agency, and constitutional failure.

## Evidence

- The source is philosophical and conceptual, not an empirical or formal proof source.
- It can support author-intent, alignment vocabulary, and governance framing.
- It should not be used as external evidence for metaphysical, psychological, or political claims without additional sources.

## Failure Modes

- Letting metaphysical language overrun operational governance requirements.
- Treating AI-managed deliberation as legitimate without exit, appeal, audit, and contestation.
- Collapsing human dignity into system utility.
- Using philosophical coherence as a substitute for institutional design.
