## Thesis

Project Theseus is a local-first, report-driven Ratcheting Modular Intelligence implementation reference. Its central design law is that the frontier should move, the floor should hold, and the system should remain small until evidence justifies growth. It combines a compact substrate, autonomy control loop, specialist routing, benchmark and residual ledgers, sparse teacher bootstrapping, checkpoints, and a trusted-machine Hive into one bounded improvement process.

## Mechanisms

- Organize growth as pressure, attempt, residual, diagnosis, compression, verification, structure, and renewed pressure.
- Use SymLiquid as the compact local substrate and SparkStream as the measurable autonomy control plane.
- Route work through an Octopus head router and bounded specialist arms with schemas, permissions, local memory, benchmarks, residuals, reliability scores, lifecycle state, and retirement criteria.
- Preserve benchmark floors through frontier, diagnostic, regression, public-calibration, and retired benchmark states.
- Keep residual escrow as a visible tail of unsolved cases rather than erasing failures after promotion.
- Close repeated successful trajectories into verified tools only after parameter, precondition, postcondition, verification, risk, and retirement criteria are recorded.
- Bound sparse teacher use through local-evidence triggers, branch isolation, audit traces, and regression gates.
- Treat Hive nodes as registered task-kind providers rather than arbitrary remote shells.

## Failure Modes

- Benchmark gaming, public-calibration leakage, or treating a benchmark score as broad capability.
- Tail erasure, tail obsession, or unstructured residual accumulation.
- Specialist-arm bloat, tool bloat, bad routing, and hidden monolith drift.
- Teacher dependence or teacher outputs becoming benchmark answers or promotion evidence.
- Unsafe public compute, arbitrary remote execution, uncontrolled update rights, or excessive power accumulation.
- Premature architecture change before data, training, inference, routing, tool, or benchmark-adapter interventions are exhausted.
