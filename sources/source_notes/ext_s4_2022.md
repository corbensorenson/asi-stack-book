## Thesis

S4 parameterizes a continuous-time state-space sequence model so its long-range
convolution can be computed efficiently, supplying a key technical ancestor of
the selective-state-space family.

## Mechanisms

- Linear state-space dynamics with a structured state matrix.
- Low-rank correction, stable diagonalization, and Cauchy-kernel computation.
- Recurrent and convolutional views of the same bounded sequence operator.

## Evidence

The paper reports long-range, image, language, and generation results, including
Long Range Arena outcomes. This repository has not reproduced its model,
benchmarks, throughput, numerical behavior, or hardware measurements.

## Failure Modes

- Long-range benchmark success can be mistaken for exact associative recall.
- A mathematically compact state can discard identity-bearing information.
- Reported asymptotic and generation gains can depend on kernels, precision, and hardware.
