## Thesis

This source is a governance comparator for the book's treatment of contestability. It helps keep fork, exit, audit, appeal, dissent, and review rights grounded in an external design literature rather than only in ASI Stack vocabulary.

## Mechanisms

- Treat contestability as a design property of AI systems and their surrounding processes.
- Emphasize the ability to challenge or dispute AI-mediated decisions.
- Provide external framing for appeal, audit, transparency, and institutional response surfaces.
- Connect value-conflict residuals to usable challenge and review interfaces.

## Evidence

- The source contributes external contestable-AI design framing.
- This repository has not implemented the framework, run a case study, or validated any ASI Stack governance interface against it.
- Use it as external literature for governance design comparison, not as evidence that the book's fork/exit/audit mechanisms are institutionally adequate.

## Failure Modes

- Contestability becomes symbolic if users cannot actually challenge a decision before the relevant harm.
- Audit logs can exist without meaningful appeal, redress, or authority narrowing.
- Institutional contestability may not transfer cleanly to self-improving AI systems without runtime and evidence records.
