## Thesis

Advanced AI interactions require game-theoretic treatment of cooperation,
commitment, bargaining, information, and institutional structure. Cooperation
is not universally good: in competitive markets or against humans it can
become collusion.

## Mechanisms

- Build a research agenda for the game theory of advanced agents.
- Examine cooperation across one-shot, repeated, bargaining, and commitment
  settings.
- Distinguish beneficial collective action from harmful coordination.

## Evidence

The source is a conceptual research agenda. It organizes questions but does not
solve multi-agent coordination or establish a local population-level result.

## Failure Modes

- Rewarding cooperation without measuring externalities.
- Tacit or explicit collusion.
- Commitment advantages creating power concentration.
- Game models omitting human or institutional participants.
