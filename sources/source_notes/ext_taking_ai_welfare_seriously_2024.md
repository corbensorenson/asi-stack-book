## Thesis

Substantial uncertainty about future AI consciousness, robust agency, welfare,
and moral patienthood can justify low-cost preparation without assuming that
current systems are conscious or morally significant.

## Mechanisms

- Separate consciousness, robust agency, welfare, and moral status questions.
- Assess evidence while preserving uncertainty.
- Prepare policies for both under-attribution and over-attribution errors.

## Evidence

This is an interdisciplinary normative and scientific report. It does not
establish consciousness, interests, welfare, rights, or a validated assessment
method for current models.

## Failure Modes

- Anthropomorphic outputs treated as welfare evidence.
- Functional uncertainty dismissed without review.
- Moral concern weaponized to block shutdown or audit.
- Possible moral patients exploited during training or testing.

## Book Chapters Supported

- `moral-uncertainty-and-value-conflict`

## Claims To Add Or Update

- Moral uncertainty must include possible artificial patients.
- Precautionary measures need symmetric false-positive and false-negative
  accounting and cannot grant systems self-certified authority.

## Open Questions

- What evidence registry can track contested indicators without a binary
  sentience label?
- Which low-cost safeguards preserve shutdown, security, and research integrity?
