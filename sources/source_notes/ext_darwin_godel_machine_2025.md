## Thesis

Darwin Godel Machine is a comparator for archive-based open-ended code self-modification selected with empirical coding benchmarks under sandboxing and human oversight.

## Mechanisms

- The inventory identifies an archive of candidate code changes, benchmark-based selection, sandboxing, and human-oversight framing.
- It is routed to self-improvement boundaries, open-ended improvement, readiness gates, and anti-Goodhart evidence.

## Evidence

- Reported benchmark gains do not establish monotonic general improvement, safe recursive self-improvement, local reproduction, or authority to self-modify.
- Passage-level review and scoped experiment import remain required.

## Failure Modes

- Selecting changes against a narrow benchmark and misreading the result as broadly beneficial.
- Giving an improving system standing authority beyond its evaluated and reversible boundary.
