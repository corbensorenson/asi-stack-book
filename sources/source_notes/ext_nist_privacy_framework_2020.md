## Thesis

Privacy risk arises from data processing across the lifecycle and is not a
subset of cybersecurity risk. Core, Profiles, and Tiers connect outcomes,
roles, and risk management without acting as law or certification.

## Mechanisms

- Identify-P, Govern-P, Control-P, Communicate-P, and Protect-P outcomes.
- Current/target Profiles, implementation Tiers, and data-action risk analysis.

## Evidence

Official NIST guidance explicitly distinguishes ordinary-processing privacy
problems from cybersecurity incidents. It reports no ASI Stack outcome and has
no force of law.

## Failure Modes

- Treating security as complete privacy protection.
- Treating a voluntary framework as law or certification.
- Selecting outcomes without testing whether they alter harms.

## Claims To Add Or Update

- Separate authorized-processing privacy risk from unauthorized-access risk.
- Preserve the framework's voluntary, non-legal ceiling.
