## Thesis

Universal Transformers are a comparator for shared-weight depth recurrence, parallel self-attention, and per-position dynamic halting.

## Mechanisms

- The inventory identifies recurrent shared depth, attention, and dynamic halting as the relevant architectural mechanisms.
- It is routed to governed deliberation, mathematical/search substrates, and cyclic-memory discussions.

## Evidence

- Benchmark results and theoretical expressivity do not establish stable deep recurrence, efficient scaling, local reproduction, or the book’s cyclic-memory claims.
- Passage review is still required for any precise technical comparison.

## Failure Modes
