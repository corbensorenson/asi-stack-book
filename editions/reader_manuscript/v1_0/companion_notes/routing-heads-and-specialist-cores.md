## Reader Promise

After reading or hearing this companion note, a human should be able to follow
why the route is the stack's unit of bounded capability selection. The router
does not merely pick what looks relevant. It records which specialist may act,
under which authority, with which readiness state, and with which fallback.

## Dense Material Routed Here

| Dense item | Plain meaning | Boundary |
|---|---|---|
| Specialist Registry Record | The record of a specialist's maximum task types, memory, tools, authority, cost, readiness, fallbacks, and evidence. | Registry capacity is an upper envelope, not automatic task authority. |
| Routing Decision Record | The task-local record of selected specialist, rejected candidates, authority subset, context, tools, cost-quality rationale, fallback, residuals, and evidence refs. | A decision is a temporary lease, not a standing appointment. |
| Route receipt | The evidence that a route was selected under the declared constraints. | A receipt does not prove the selected specialist is empirically best. |
| Rejected alternatives | The cheaper, stronger, stale, overprivileged, underqualified, or unsafe candidates that were refused. | Rejection reasons are audit evidence, not clutter. |
| Readiness and fallback | The current qualification state and the path when the route fails or expires. | Plausible capability is not enough without readiness and fallback. |
| MoECOT runtime crosswalk | A source-bound implementation-reference vocabulary for routed specialist lanes and control-plane ledgers. | It is not local runtime reproduction unless replay artifacts are imported and validated. |

## Main Spine Must Keep

The reader chapter should not move these boundaries exclusively into companion
material:

- routing is assignment under constraint, not load balancing alone;
- a selected route grants only a task-local subset of the specialist envelope;
- excess authority, missing readiness, missing context, unsafe tools, stale
  evidence, and hidden residuals block or narrow a route;
- rejected alternatives and non-selection reasons are part of the evidence;
- no specialist-routing benchmark, learned-router quality result, runtime
  route, MoECOT replay, orchestration benchmark, route-quality measurement, or
  deployed authority-enforcement test is claimed.

## Audio Treatment

In an audio script, do not read every registry or routing-decision field as a
catalog. Narrate routing as a lease:

- a plan asks for bounded capability;
- the registry names possible specialists;
- the decision records why one specialist is allowed and why others are not;
- authority is narrowed for the task;
- fallback and residuals stay attached after the route.

Detailed route-shape vocabulary and MoECOT crosswalk language can be routed to
this companion note. The main audio should keep the ordinary reader focused on
the question: why this specialist, under this envelope, right now?
