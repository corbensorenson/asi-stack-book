## Reader Promise

After reading or hearing this companion note, a human should be able to follow
why the chapter is friendly to cyclic methods but hostile to cyclic
laundering. A cyclic adapter, phase feature, RoPE receipt, circulant mixer, or
block-cyclic route can be structurally interesting and still fail as a useful
model component.

## Main Spine Must Keep

The reader chapter should not move these boundaries exclusively into companion
material:

- cyclic structure is allowed only where a receipt, diagnostic, or benchmark
  says it is present;
- parameter reduction, structural exactness, or finite RoPE distinguishability
  does not imply model quality, long-context performance, speed, memory savings,
  training stability, hardware benefit, or deployment readiness;
- baseline symmetry and negative controls are mandatory before adoption;
- failed cyclic substrates become negative evidence and fallback requirements;
- no CoilRA quality result, MultiCoil RoPE quality result, cyclic mixer
  benchmark, RoPE context-length result, hardware-kernel benchmark,
  training-stability result, or support-state promotion is claimed.

## Audio Treatment

In an audio script, do not read the cyclic-method glossary as a proof catalog.
Narrate the chapter as adoption discipline:

- cyclic ideas can be structurally real;
- structural reality is not usefulness;
- ordinary baselines must be treated symmetrically;
- tradeoffs keep ledgers separate;
- canary adoption and fallback come before default routing.

Detailed phase, winding, receipt, baseline, and diagnostic vocabulary can be
routed to this companion note. The main audio should keep the ordinary reader
focused on the rule: cyclic structure has to earn its route.
