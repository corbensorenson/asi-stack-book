## Scalable Oversight and Adversarial AI Control

### Distinct responsibility and falsifier

The chapter owns the admission contract for interpreting limited supervision,
not the downstream claim, training, release, or execution decision. Its core
design fails to add value if a protocol record cannot distinguish a helpful
bounded result from missing access, correlated review, a weak baseline, an
absent outcome audit, or an unowned residual. Net protocol benefit remains an
unrun empirical question.

### Audited comparator delta

- `ext_weak_to_strong_generalization_2023` supplies weak/strong envelopes,
  held-out outcomes, ceiling comparison, partial recovery, and explicit
  disanalogies.
- `ext_ai_safety_debate_2018` supplies precommitment, adversarial evidence
  selection, bounded decomposition, judge instructions, justified ignorance,
  the idealized game analogy, and its judge/equilibrium/stochastic limitations.
- `ext_scalable_oversight_weak_llms_2024` supplies empirical weak-judge direct
  QA, consultancy, debate, access-condition, and persuasion comparisons with an
  explicit inference-to-training boundary.

The chapter does not claim a winning protocol. Its delta is a typed,
consumer-bound artifact that keeps baseline, audit, dependency graph, residual,
expiry, authority separation, and refusal semantics attached downstream.

### Derived consequences

1. Oversight adequacy depends on the downstream consumer; test selection,
   training, evidence transition, and release cannot share one admission rule.
2. Abstention needs evidence and a defeater, or it can become strategic opacity
   or a human-queue denial of service.
3. Review diversity is a dependency graph rather than a count of role labels.
4. Additional review consumes latency, privacy, evaluator, and operator budget;
   useful findings and governance cost must be reported together.

### Strongest objections

The chapter directly handles the unsolved-judge objection, in-band evaluator
capture, the claim that compound systems already provide critics/judges, and
the governance-tax objection. It concedes the surviving limits: records do not
create competence; fully captured evaluation paths may force refusal; existing
orchestration can be the substrate; and net useful-throughput benefit is
unestablished until a preregistered natural-work campaign runs.

### Worked trace and evidence route
