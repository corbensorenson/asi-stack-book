# Data Engine custody, update, and deletion lifecycle refinement

This refinement joins three existing bounded evidence surfaces without broadening them: the four-scenario admission probe, the 24-surface/15-transaction full-state bridge, and the three-seed/four-arm update-causality campaign. The independent consumer reruns all three validators, reaches all 82 routes in an eight-stage custody lifecycle, and rejects 96/96 identity, gate, ordering, replay, claim-laundering, support, and external-effect mutations.

The stages are scope, admission, full-state binding, update receipt, deletion assessment, claim-axis adjudication, bounded custody, and material-change readmission. Identity custody binds datum, cohort, provenance, rights, authority, split, dataset, model, base and selected checkpoints, optimizer, scheduler, RNG, cache, backup, lineage, descendants, deletion request, evaluator, consumer, and version. Behavioral change, causal influence reduction, privacy protection or erasure, legal compliance, lineage invalidation, physical storage erasure, backup erasure, and external-descendant closure remain different statuses.

The complete witness emits one bounded custody record and returns only to version-2 scoped admission after invalidation, ordinary-route blocking, a rerun requirement, and a rollback or revocation receipt. Support-state and external-effect authority remain exactly `none`. The result is at `experiments/data_engine_lifecycle_refinement/results/2026-07-16-local.json`; reproduce it with `python3 scripts/validate_data_engine_lifecycle_refinement.py`.

This is finite authored custody logic over small local results. It does not establish source truth, semantic contamination control, dataset quality, rights validity, foundation-model continual learning, causal influence removal, forgetting, privacy/legal/storage/backup erasure, external-descendant closure, semantic or production rollback, capability, safety, readiness, release, transfer, SOTA, AGI, or ASI.
