## Outcome

`AsiStackProofs.TribunalRefinement` replaces assumed blocking policies and
literal route examples with a reachable seven-stage lifecycle: idle, requested,
dossier-bound, panel-run, verdict-issued, consumer-acknowledged, and appeal-
resolved. It binds the exact case and target versions, evidence and dossier
digests, panel and policy identities, consumer, verdict version, event order,
panel discipline, verdict artifact, owner handoff, acknowledgment, and appeal.

High-risk review requires adversarial probes and a sufficiently sized panel.
Declared method independence also requires distinct groups, an acyclic graph,
and shared-evidence-risk disclosure; this remains record discipline, not proof
of independence or competence. Falsification, abstention, veto, dissent,
actions, constraints, residuals, and appeal remain first-class. Reused verdicts
cannot cross changed evidence, default approval is rejected, and a support-
change request can only hand off to the Evidence owner. The Tribunal cannot
assign support or commit an external effect.

`python3 scripts/validate_tribunal_refinement.py` independently recomputes all
twenty-eight routes, consumes the exact three-valid/five-invalid tribunal-review
suite and one-valid/eleven-invalid five-project method-independence lifecycle,
executes the six-event witness, and rejects all 45 mutations.

## Exact boundary

This is finite authored structured-record evidence. Reviewer competence,
independence in fact, error decorrelation, dossier completeness, evidence truth,
probe or falsification quality, verdict correctness, consensus quality,
institutional legitimacy, fair process, action or constraint efficacy, appeal
quality, claim truth, evidence adequacy, useful advantage, causality, safety,
cost advantage, live affected-party outcomes, deployment, reproduction, and
transfer are not established. The final verdict and owner handoff create no
chapter support movement, external effect, release authority, SOTA result, AGI
claim, or ASI claim.
