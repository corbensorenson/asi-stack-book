## Current-comparator correction

The 2026-07-16 primary-source refresh changed the strongest recurrent
comparator. Mamba-3 remains a current selective-state-space mechanism and
official code reference, but Gated DeltaNet-2 (arXiv:2605.22791) reports stronger
aggregate results than the named Mamba-family variants on its 1.3B/100B-token
language, commonsense, retrieval, and RULER envelope. The book must therefore
name Gated DeltaNet-2 in the chapter and source appendix rather than freezing
the March 2026 frontier in place.

Inkling is retained as a current open-weight hybrid stress comparator, not a
dense-Transformer baseline. Its provider reports 975B total/41B active
parameters and hardware floors of 2 TB aggregate VRAM for BF16 or 600 GB for
NVFP4. The local host has 16 GB unified Apple M1 memory, no NVIDIA/CUDA path,
and 116 GiB free disk at preflight. API use would not expose the weights,
mutable state, energy, or component-level ablations required by the frozen
comparison.

Mamba-3's official path requires Linux, and the reported GPU route requires
NVIDIA/CUDA. The official repository did not expose a frozen end-to-end
Mamba-3 language-model checkpoint sufficient for the paper result at the
refresh boundary. The archived TRM repository exposes no release checkpoint;
its smallest documented training route assumes one 48 GB L40S for roughly
18–20 hours, while its ARC routes assume four H100 GPUs for roughly three days.

DreamCoder's official repository is reachable and a mechanism proxy may be
possible on CPU. Running it alone would not reproduce the frozen tournament:
there is no OneCell implementation, no shared Cognitive Kernel ABI workload,
no matched adapters, and no complete total-lifecycle instrumentation. It is
therefore retained as an exact-search/program-learning comparator and not
laundered into a SOTA challenge.
