## Purpose and closure rule

This record tests whether the two July 2026 Corben papers were mined rather
than merely summarized. Every substantive section family must terminate in at
least one of four states:

1. **chapter integration** — the mechanism, invariant, boundary, or failure was
   added to an existing chapter owner;
2. **source-note integration** — the material is preserved for drafting,
   limitations, or terminology without duplicating chapter prose;
3. **research obligation** — an algorithm, artifact, experiment, comparator,
   or unresolved claim is routed to the roadmap and research backlog;
4. **explicit boundary** — related work, non-goals, philosophical claims, or
   illustrative schemas are retained without being presented as book evidence.

No row may terminate only in the inventory, Appendix G, or a claim of having
read the paper. The raw Markdown is the canonical mining source; the supplied
DOCX is a verified presentation counterpart. All paper-derived support remains
`argument`.

## Chapter-boundary decision

The audit strengthens existing owners rather than adding chapters. DCC is a
compilation and lifecycle joining existing capability, compiler, routing,
verification, runtime, memory, provenance, readiness, substrate, roadmap, and
RSI layers. PWM is a semantic constitution joining existing claim, context,
planning, grounding, security, provenance, federation, learning, proof,
roadmap, and integration layers. A paper-shaped chapter would duplicate those
responsibilities.

Reconsider only after implementation exposes either:

- a stable NCO/foundry interface with authority and evidence that no current
  chapter can own without conflict; or
- a trusted semantic-kernel interface with a distinct reader job and evidence
  program that cannot be explained through Claim Ledgers, Context ABI,
  Security Kernel, Planning, and the Integrated Architecture.
