# QCSA Implementation And Evidence Reconciliation

Date: 2026-07-13

Status: P1–P4 complete; bounded evidence reconciled without chapter-core
promotion or a new chapter

## Decision

Question-Compiled Semantic Addressing now has three distinct repository roles:

1. `sources/source_notes/qcsa_whitepaper.md` records the Corben-authored design
   source and preserves its source-level conceptual boundary.
2. `experiments/qcsa_reference/` contains a deterministic twelve-lane local
   implementation and frozen synthetic evaluation.
3. `experiments/qcsa_vertical_reference/` contains one governed 13-stage trace
   with a real temporary-file effect, independent byte observation, same-SOID
   migration, and exact rollback.

These roles improve nine existing chapter owners. They do not warrant a new
chapter: QCSA remains an interface contract spanning identity, context,
evidence, routing, authority, migration, residuals, and integration rather than
a distinct chapter thesis. The strongest preregistered claim—matched-resource
advantage—failed on the frozen proxy, while the active-question ablation is N2
proxy/regime evidence rather than an exact or broad refutation.

## Disposition Accounting

`claim_decisions/qcsa_reference_evaluation_dispositions.json` records ten
non-core decisions: five bounded `promote` recommendations, two `narrow`, two
`refute`, and one `no_change`. “Promote” means eligible for the ordinary
evidence-transition review process; it is not an automatic support-state
change. No QCSA upward transition has been accepted in this reconciliation.

The five review candidates are plural-facet fixture value, identity-indirection
migration value, certificate/residual/authority-field fixture value, explicit
migration-compatibility fixture value, and task-calibration performance on the
exact corpus. Active-question value and exact matched advantage are refuted for
the frozen corpus. Semantic preservation and governance prevention are
narrowed. Open-world or production transfer remains unchanged.

All nine chapter-core decisions are `no_change`; all remain at `argument`.
`evidence_quality/core_claim_vectors.json` now attaches the QCSA result and
vertical trace to those nine claims while explicitly retaining internal-only
independence, unmeasured broad coverage, unassessed construct validity, and
unestablished transfer.
