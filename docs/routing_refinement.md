# Routing and replaceable-substrate refinement

**State:** finite authored lifecycle validated; no chapter-core support change.
**Receipt:** `experiments/routing_refinement/results/2026-07-15-local.json`

This refinement makes the routing chapter's architectural claim precise without confusing control-plane correctness with model capability. The Lean model and independently implemented Python consumer bind one task request to one frozen specialist denominator, one selected specialist, authority and readiness state, fresh context and tool leases, an evaluator, a policy, and a consumer. They then require distinct dispatch, outcome-observation, and revocation-aware closure stages.

The consumer covers all forty-two routes across seven reachable stages and rejects all forty-seven registered mutations. It preserves the existing routing-decision-lease suite (3 valid, 7 expected-invalid), readiness/residual suite (4 valid, 5 expected-invalid), and the post-v2 routing/deliberation result. The last remains a bounded negative result: zero fallback activations and two `no_change` claim dispositions prevent the synthetic study from being laundered into production or model-scale support.

The architecture-neutral point is narrow but important. A transformer, state-space model, KAN, recurrent system, graph system, memory-augmented model, or future substrate can occupy a specialist slot only if it satisfies the same typed capability, authority, readiness, lease, dispatch, observation, and retirement interface. The proof does not say which substrate is best, that routing is useful on natural tasks, or that a selected route produces a correct answer. Route quality and answer quality are separately observed fields.

## Exact boundary

- Proven in the authored model: identity preservation, event-order enforcement, candidate-denominator custody, label-leak rejection, authority/readiness/fallback/residual gating, ambiguity-triggered selective action, separate dispatch authority, separate route and answer outcome recording, revocation-aware closure, and absence of support/effect authority.
- Rechecked from existing suites: 3/7 routing lease fixtures, 4/5 readiness/residual fixtures, 900 routing records, 540 deliberation records, 15 known extra-compute harms, and two no-change dispositions.
- Not established: natural useful routing, strong-model transfer, production deployment, runtime or replay correctness, substrate superiority, recursive architectural self-improvement, or chapter-core support.
