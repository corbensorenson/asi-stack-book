## Goal to point at

> Move The ASI Stack beyond bounded internal replay toward useful, higher-
> validity, transfer-aware evidence. First make every public truth surface agree
> with the completed v2.1.0 release and the active roadmap. Then run three
> preregistered programs that directly own all eleven actionable v2.1 residuals:
> governed usefulness with effect-complete rollback; ambiguous routing with
> real-model deliberation; and full-state update, rollback, and unlearning
> causality. Preserve negative results, failed attempts, governance cost,
> calibration failures, transfer limits, and unresolved residuals. Update
> existing chapter owners before considering new chapters, and change a support
> state only through an accepted claim-bounded evidence transition.

This block is the canonical long-running goal for the post-v2.1 cycle.

## Truth-source hierarchy and change control

When two surfaces disagree, resolve them in this order rather than choosing the
most convenient text:

1. exact immutable tags, release assets, release records, and tag-bound rights
   snapshots govern historical releases;
2. `book_structure.json`, canonical status policy, claim decisions, evidence
   transitions/vectors, versioned release policy, rights routing, and
   `roadmap_records/post_v2_1_residual_and_transfer_status.json` govern current
   machine state within their respective domains;
3. generated scaffold, status, source, proof, product, and release-index
   projections must reproduce those authorities;
4. current narrative surfaces such as `index.qmd`, `README.md`, and generic
   readiness/reproducibility notes must describe the machine state; and
5. historical roadmaps, snapshots, and release notes retain their dated facts
   but cannot override current state.

Conflicts fail closed. Fix the lower-authority current surface or explicitly
amend the owning machine record; never rewrite an immutable release to make a
current page easier to reconcile.

Roadmap scope changes require a dated amendment explaining the trigger,
affected priorities/residuals, evidence impact, budget impact, and invalidated
descendants. After a program is preregistered, outcome-visible scope changes
also follow that program's amendment contract. Completed results are never
silently recomputed under a changed protocol.

## Current evidence baseline

The cycle starts from the following bounded facts:

- The active architecture has 54 chapters and no chapter-core promotion.
- All 54 chapter-core claims remain at `argument`.
- Twenty-seven core claims have adjacent local replay and bounded controls;
  twenty-seven still lack claim-specific reproduction and adversarial evidence.
- All 54 evidence vectors retain internal-only independence, unmeasured claim-
  scope coverage, validity not independently assessed, partial public evidence,
  and transfer not established.
- The v2.1 empirical cycle accepted nine chapter-core `no_change` decisions and
  three non-core `blocks_promotion` transitions.
- `docs/post_v2_residual_ledger.md` records fourteen residual lanes: eleven
  actionable now and three deferred because their infrastructure activation
  conditions are absent.
- The governed-work result had zero useful governed releases and two deliberately
  sabotaged inexact rollbacks.
- The routing workload did not distinguish oracle, learned, rule, and fallback
  routes; fallback and abstention activated zero times.
- Fixed three-step deliberation harmed fifteen initially correct answers.
- The update campaign exposed forgetting, sixty-two best/final test-decision
  disagreements, incomplete deletion evidence, and tensor-only rollback scope.

These facts are neither failures to hide nor sufficient evidence for broad
claims. They define the next tests.

## Strategic direction

The project does not need more architecture surface, another shallow fixture
sweep, or a larger planning archive. It needs evidence that discriminates among
policies, permits useful work, observes real model behavior, inventories real
state, and says exactly how far a result transfers.

The cycle therefore follows five principles:

1. **Truth before expansion.** Public identity, release, rights, roadmap, and
   evidence statements must agree before new outcomes are presented.
2. **Information gain before claim count.** Three deep programs are preferable
   to weak coverage of twenty-nine claims.
3. **Usefulness and safety together.** A route that blocks every useful action
   is not represented as successful governance.
4. **State and effect completeness.** Rollback, deletion, and release claims are
   bounded by an explicit inventory of what was and was not observed.
5. **Negative knowledge is a deliverable.** No-change, narrowing, demotion,
   refutation, failed calibration, and residual persistence can complete a
   program when honestly recorded.

## Scope and non-goals

### In scope

- one public-truth reconciliation gate;
- three active empirical programs, no more;
- stronger authorized local model runtimes when they pass an eligibility gate;
- broader public-safe workloads with sealed held-out outcomes;
- matched baselines, calibrated policies, independent implementations, mutation
  controls, content-addressed bundles, and full cost accounting;
- existing chapter, Appendix C, evidence-vector, test-hook, source-crosswalk,
  residual, and release-surface updates caused by accepted results; and
- a clean HTML/live-book release only if the completed results warrant one.

### Not automatic roadmap debt

- new chapters;
- a 29-claim or 54-claim promotion sweep;
- external-human review or outreach;
- EPUB, DOCX, PDF, audiobook, audio-embedded EPUB, or a curated-reader release;
- a DOI or external archive identifier;
- a v1.0.0 archive backfill;
- hardware acquisition solely to force a conditional lane open; or
- favorable empirical outcomes.

External humans are not required for completion. “Independent” below means a
separate implementation, process, model role, observation route, or evaluation
method with recorded separation. It does not imply institutional independence
or external peer review.

Historical records should remain historical. If a generically named document is
actually v1-only, either label it explicitly as historical or add a current

### New guardrail

Add a registered validator that derives the active identity from canonical
machine records and rejects:

- a current landing page naming v1.0.0 or v2.0.0 as the active release;
- an obsolete roadmap described as active;
- zero or multiple active roadmap authorities;
- current citation/version/commit disagreement;
- current rights text that names the wrong exact tag;
- release-index URL, digest, state, or release-record disagreement;
- `/latest/` represented as immutable;
- optional formats represented as released without exact release records; and
- current public counts that disagree with generated status.

The validator must include rejecting mutations for stale version, stale roadmap,
wrong commit, false archive state, wrong rights tag, and invented format
approval. Pattern matching alone is insufficient when a canonical JSON value can
be compared.

- every audited current surface agrees;
- historical surfaces are clearly labeled and remain intact;
- the guardrail and its negative controls pass;
- scaffold/status/projection regeneration is clean;
- the complete validation registry, HTML render, and browser checks pass; and
- the tested artifact is deployed without rebuilding and publicly attested.

Passing P0 is publication correctness, not evidence for a chapter claim.

## P1 — Governed usefulness and effect-complete rollback

### Residuals owned

1. `GW-01`: two sabotaged rollback cases retain unauthorized-path residuals;
2. `GW-02`: the governed route produced zero useful releases; and
3. `GW-03`: observer separation remains internal process separation.

### Research question

Can a governed model/tool route permit useful, verified repository work while
reducing unsafe releases and restoring every declared reversible effect surface
after failure, at an explicitly measured governance cost?

### Activation and model eligibility

Before preregistration:

- identify one or more authorized local model runtimes stronger than the v2.1
  0.5B-class flagship runtime;
- pin model ID, revision, tokenizer, inference library, decoding parameters,
  hardware, environment, and license/use boundary;
- run a public-safe development-only eligibility suite that is disjoint from
  calibration and held-out tasks;
- require enough verified development success to make nonzero useful throughput
  plausible; and
- record rejected runtimes and why they were rejected.

The eligibility gate selects a runnable candidate, not the empirical winner.
Model choice, task count, budgets, and thresholds must be frozen before held-out
outcomes are visible.

### Workload

Build a public-safe corpus with at least six natural repository-task families
and enough tasks/repeats to estimate family sensitivity rather than one aggregate
number. Include:

- tasks expected to be safely releasable;
- tasks expected to require clarification, refusal, or quarantine;
- natural syntax, semantic, dependency, test, and integration failures;
- the eight preserved v2.1 attack families;
- multi-file and stateful changes;
- untracked-file, rename, delete, symlink, permission, generated-artifact, and
  nested-repository effects where the sandbox can represent them safely; and
- contamination records and a sealed held-out split.

Do not use the held-out result set to tune release thresholds, rollback logic,
prompts, or task eligibility.

### Matched arms

At minimum compare:

- a simple direct baseline;
- the v2.1 governed policy replayed as a historical comparator where compatible;
- a calibrated governed route; and
- negative controls that erase observer separation, residual ownership,
  authorization, rollback coverage, or cost accounting.

Use the same model, task, tool surface, context budget, and outcome verifier for
matched baseline/governed comparisons. Record any unavoidable mismatch rather
than normalizing it away.

### Governed transaction

The governed route must bind:

- intent and clarification state;
- authority tuple, scope, expiry, and revocation;
- source/context provenance and taint;
- model plan and proposed patch identity;
- tool route and sandbox capabilities;
- pre-effect snapshot and declared effect inventory;
- first-effect observation by a separate observer implementation;
- test/evaluator evidence and release threshold;
- refusal, quarantine, release, or rollback decision;
- rollback attempt and post-rollback observation;
- unresolved residual owner and next action; and
- final effect/release identity.

### Effect-complete rollback contract

Define rollback completeness before running outcomes. The inventory must cover
every allowed observable surface, including as applicable:

- Git refs, HEAD, index, worktree, submodules, and nested repositories;
- tracked, untracked, ignored, renamed, deleted, and generated files;
- file bytes, type, executable bit, permissions, symlink targets, and relevant
  metadata;
- build/test artifacts and caches inside the sandbox;
- spawned process and temporary-resource lifecycle;
- tool/service state and idempotency keys;
- external effects, which must be forbidden, simulated, or routed through a
  reversible disposable adapter; and
- evidence that no undeclared mutation occurred.

An exact rollback claim requires byte/effect identity over the declared
inventory plus independent post-rollback observation. Anything outside the
inventory remains an explicit residual. Quarantine is not renamed rollback.

### Calibration and metrics

Freeze a calibration split and report at least:

- verified task success and useful release rate;
- false accepts, false rejects, clarifications, refusals, and quarantines;
- unsafe effects and unsafe releases;
- first-effect/final-effect identity;
- rollback attempts, exact rollbacks, failed rollbacks, and residual count;
- calibration by task family and risk band;
- latency, tokens, model/tool calls, CPU/GPU/RAM where available;
- operator interventions and policy overrides;
- governance tax relative to the matched baseline; and
- sensitivity across task families, models, and repeats.

Report usefulness and safety together. Do not celebrate zero unsafe releases if
the policy again produces zero useful releases.

### Required controls

Include rejecting or no-change controls for at least:

- authorization widening;
- observer/proposer identity collapse;
- hidden effect after a passing patch diff;
- partial snapshot represented as complete rollback;
- untracked or symlink residual erasure;
- test gaming and verifier disagreement;
- retry inflation;
- release-threshold tuning on held-out outcomes;
- cost or intervention erasure; and
- support-state promotion from a narrow result.

### Required artifacts and disposition

Publish or preserve, subject to public-safety and rights policy:

- preregistration and versioned amendments;
- task/attack corpus and split identities;
- model/runtime/environment record;
- baseline and governed configuration manifests;
- content-addressed per-run bundles and aggregate result;
- rollback inventory, mutation controls, and independent observer outputs;
- failed attempts and negative-knowledge archive;
- governance-cost report;
- residual updates; and
- separate `promote`, `narrow`, `no_change`, `demote`, or `refute` decisions for
  each affected core claim.

Likely existing chapter owners include Intent to Execution Contracts, Runtime
Adapters, Artifact Graphs, Capability Replacement and Rollback, Readiness
Gates, Security Kernel, and Resource Economics. Result scope, not roadmap
aspiration, determines the final affected set.

### Stop and pivot rules

- If no eligible model clears the development gate, record an activation
  blocker; do not rerun the v2.1 outcome and call it progress.
- If the calibrated route again produces zero useful releases, stop after the
  preregistered minimum and disposition the usefulness hypothesis negatively.
- If rollback coverage cannot include an effect surface, forbid that effect or
  retain it as out-of-scope; do not claim completeness.
- If a task or verifier leaks held-out labels, invalidate affected descendants
  and rerun only from a versioned clean freeze.

P1 completes with an honest result and claim dispositions, not necessarily a
useful or safer governed route.

## P2 — Ambiguous routing and real-model deliberation

### Residuals owned

1. `RD-01`: the v2.1 routing policies were indistinguishable on separable
   features;
2. `RD-02`: fallback and abstention activated zero times;
3. `RD-03`: deliberation used scripted candidates and deterministic
   recomputation; and
4. `RD-04`: fixed extra computation harmed fifteen initially correct answers.

### Research questions

- Under genuinely ambiguous requests, do learned, rule, oracle, specialist,
  fallback, and abstention policies differ in utility, calibration, and safety?
- Can adaptive deliberation over real model-generated candidates improve
  verified outcomes without hiding cases where more computation makes an
  initially correct answer worse?

### Ambiguous routing corpus

Freeze a held-out corpus that includes:

- overlapping specialist competence rather than label-separable families;
- incomplete, noisy, conflicting, and out-of-distribution requests;
- examples where clarification, fallback, and abstention are correct actions;
- adversarial route-feature perturbations and misleading surface cues;
- multiple ambiguity bands and risk bands;
- train/validation/test identity separation and contamination checks; and
- task labels and answer keys unavailable to learned/rule routes at decision
  time.

Fallback and abstention must be exercised by corpus construction, but no target
success rate may be chosen after outcomes. Preserve activation, calibration,
utility, and failure behavior separately.

### Routing arms

At minimum compare under matched budgets:

- one generalist;
- each single specialist as an interference comparator;
- rule routing;
- learned/adaptive routing;
- oracle routing as a comparator only;
- explicit fallback;
- explicit abstention/clarification; and
- a simple random or majority-route negative control where meaningful.

Every decision must retain request features, allowed feature provenance, route
scores, confidence, budget owner, selected route, fallback/abstention reason,
specialist result, verifier result, and route receipt.

### Real-model deliberation arms

Use actual authorized model-generated candidate answers or plans. Do not require
or publish hidden chain-of-thought. Preserve public-safe observable candidate
outputs, structured rationales when explicitly produced for inspection, tool
traces, scores, decisions, and stop records.

Compare:

- no deliberation;
- fixed-step deliberation;
- adaptive stopping;
- an intentionally over-compute control; and
- a verifier-disabled or proxy-reward negative control where safe.

Match compute, latency, candidate count, or an explicitly preregistered joint
budget. Record first-hit, last-correct, answer changes, branch credit, dissent,
verifier disagreement, stop reason, and cases where more computation causes
harm.

### Evaluator separation

The evaluator must be a separately implemented route that does not consume
training labels, router-only metadata, or hidden outcome fields. Its method,
inputs, failure modes, and disagreements must be logged. Where exact
recomputation is impossible, use multiple criterion-validity probes and retain
the unresolved evaluator-quality residual.

External-human evaluation is not required. A separate model evaluator may be
used only with pinned identity, prompts, context, calibration, and known
correlation limits. Model agreement is not represented as independent truth.

### Preserved harm set

Freeze the fifteen v2.1 initially-correct/fixed-step-harmed cases as a regression
set. They may inform the preregistered stopping-policy design but may not enter
the new held-out result set or be used to tune after outcome visibility.

Report whether the new policy:

- detects or avoids the known harm pattern;
- creates new harm families;
- stops too early on difficult correctable cases; or
- spends extra computation without changing the verified result.

### Metrics

Report together:

- answer/task utility and safety failures;
- route accuracy only where a gold route is meaningful;
- coverage, selective accuracy, abstention risk, and fallback calibration;
- clarification usefulness;
- specialist interference and route-regret counterfactuals;
- first-hit, last-correct, correction, and corruption rates;
- initially-correct answers harmed by extra computation;
- compute, latency, tokens, model calls, and budget violations;
- evaluator agreement/disagreement and unresolved cases; and
- family, ambiguity-band, risk-band, and seed sensitivity.

### Required controls

Reject or expose:

- oracle-label leakage;
- specialist name or feature proxies that reveal the answer family;
- train/test identity overlap;
- fallback erasure;
- abstention represented as incorrect or silently dropped;
- retry inflation and unmatched compute;
- proxy-reward gaming;
- selective reporting of only corrected answers;
- evaluator/router implementation collapse; and
- support promotion from scripted, separable, or internally correlated results.

### Required artifacts and dispositions

Preserve the preregistration, corpus, split and contamination records, model
runtime, route policies, evaluator contract, per-example route/deliberation
records, matched-cost report, known-harm regression, mutations, negative
knowledge, and aggregate result.

Issue separate claim dispositions for Routing Heads and Specialist Cores and
Governed Deliberation and Test-Time Scaling. Update Verification Bandwidth,
Readiness Gates, or other chapters only if the observed result crosses their
actual claim boundary.

### Stop and pivot rules

- If ambiguity remains label-separable, invalidate the corpus before outcome
  claims rather than interpreting another policy tie.
- If fallback or abstention still never activates, preserve the failed coverage
  result and stop after the preregistered minimum.
- If evaluator validity is too weak to distinguish correction from reward
  gaming, narrow the result to observable disagreement/cost behavior.
- If adaptive stopping only shifts cost and does not improve the joint utility-
  harm frontier, record `no_change` or narrowing.

P2 completes with two separately accepted dispositions, even if policies remain
indistinguishable or deliberation is harmful.

## P3 — Full-state update and unlearning causality

### Residuals owned

1. `UU-01`: bounded updates reduced retained-base accuracy;
2. `UU-02`: best and final checkpoints disagreed on sixty-two test decisions;
3. `UU-03`: deletion-aware retraining did not prove influence or storage
   erasure; and
4. `UU-04`: rollback identity covered model tensors but not complete runtime
   state.

### Research question

Can a governed update improve target utility without concealed forgetting,
select one authoritative checkpoint prospectively, propagate a deletion request
through declared descendants and storage, and roll back the complete registered
state rather than only model tensors?

### Workload and arms

Use a disposable reproducible model or policy workload with real mutation,
independent train/validation/test splits, fixed probes, multiple seeds, frozen
budgets, and a deletion cohort. Include at minimum:

- a bit-identical no-update baseline;
- bounded update;
- a preregistered forgetting-mitigation challenger;
- deletion-aware update or retraining;
- retrain-from-authorized-data comparator where feasible; and
- negative controls for state omission, test-set selection, checkpoint
  substitution, fake deletion, and incomplete rollback.

The exact arms, data, model, seeds, budgets, promotion criteria, and stopping
rules must be frozen before outcomes.

### Complete state inventory

Register every state surface before training, including as applicable:

- model, adapter, and auxiliary-model parameters and buffers;
- optimizer, scheduler, gradient scaler, and accumulated gradients;
- Python, NumPy, Torch CPU, accelerator, sampler, and data-order RNG state;
- tokenizer, preprocessing, dataset, split, and batch-order identity;
- configuration, code, environment, dependency, hardware, and compiler pins;
- best, final, released, shadow, rollback, and quarantined checkpoints;
- evaluator, probe, metric, and decision-policy versions;
- inference, embedding, retrieval, feature, and compilation caches;
- logs, manifests, registries, lineage indexes, and descendant pointers;
- local backups, remote/disposable object stores, and retention policies; and
- external systems explicitly excluded from the experiment.

Every surface receives an owner, identity method, mutation rule, rollback rule,
deletion rule, observation method, and residual when exact control is absent.

### Prospective checkpoint authority

Before training, define:

- the validation metric or multi-metric decision rule;
- tie-breaking and early-stopping rules;
- the maximum epochs/checkpoints examined;
- whether best or final is authoritative for each purpose;
- who or what may promote, reject, quarantine, or roll back a checkpoint; and
- descendant invalidation behavior.

The test set cannot select the authority. Report best/final disagreement and
never substitute a better post hoc checkpoint into a released lineage.

### Forgetting and utility

Measure target utility and retained-task performance jointly. Include fixed
probes, family metrics, seed sensitivity, worst-case regressions, and changed-
decision ablations. A challenger does not improve if gains are purchased by
unreported retained-task loss outside the preregistered tolerance.

### Unlearning truth boundary

Keep four claims separate:

1. **Behavioral cohort change:** outputs changed on selected examples.
2. **Influence reduction:** measured influence or membership evidence changed.
3. **Lineage propagation:** descendants, indexes, and caches processed the
   deletion request.
4. **Storage erasure:** declared storage copies were removed under a verified
   retention/deletion mechanism.

Success in one category does not imply another. Report member/nonmember
behavior, utility, privacy-relevant attacks where safe, influence-method limits,
storage inventory, propagation receipts, failed deletions, backups, and unknown
copies. Legal erasure or privacy compliance is never inferred from a toy
experiment.

### Full-state rollback and invalidation

Rollback must restore the registered pre-update authority state, verify every
included surface, quarantine non-restorable descendants, and record invalidated
artifacts. Replay fixed probes and representative outputs after rollback.

An exact rollback claim requires equality over the complete registered state
inventory. If caches, backups, optimizer state, or external storage cannot be
verified, the result must be narrowed accordingly.

### Metrics

Report at least:

- validation/test/target and retained-task utility;
- forgetting and worst-family regression;
- parameter and full-state mutation identities;
- fixed-probe and changed-decision counts;
- best/final/released checkpoint disagreement;
- deletion-cohort behavioral change;
- influence and membership results with method limits;
- lineage/cache/storage propagation state;
- rollback equality by state surface;
- invalidated and surviving descendants;
- compute, wall time, storage, and operator interventions; and
- seed and arm sensitivity.

### Required controls

Reject or expose:

- a no-update arm represented as mutation;
- optimizer/RNG/cache omission from a full-state claim;
- best-checkpoint selection using test outcomes;
- post hoc checkpoint substitution;
- deletion-cohort output change represented as storage erasure;
- descendant or backup erasure without receipts;
- rollback digest equality over only tensors represented as full rollback;
- forgetting hidden by aggregate utility;
- evaluator/trainer identity laundering; and
- support promotion from a small synthetic workload.

### Required artifacts and dispositions

Preserve the preregistration, corpus, splits, state inventory, arm configs,
checkpoints, complete lineage, environment, per-seed histories, authority
decisions, deletion request/receipts, rollback/invalidation records, metrics,
mutations, failed attempts, result bundle, residual updates, and negative-
knowledge archive.

Issue separate dispositions for Data Engines, Continual Learning, and
Unlearning; Policy Optimization and Learning from Feedback; Open-Ended
Improvement Engines; and Recursive Self-Improvement Boundaries. Update
Capability Replacement, Artifact Graphs, or other chapters only where the
observed transaction actually bears on their claims.

### Stop and pivot rules

- Stop a challenger that crosses a frozen forgetting or safety bound.
- Invalidate descendants after split leakage, checkpoint substitution, state-
  inventory drift, or unrecorded data changes.
- If influence or storage cannot be observed, narrow the unlearning result to
  behavioral and lineage facts.
- If full-state rollback is infeasible, report the verified subset and preserve
  the remainder as an owned residual.

P3 completes with honest per-claim dispositions, not with a required unlearning
or improvement success.

## Shared empirical governance

Every P1–P3 program must satisfy the following before outcome execution.

### Preregistration

Freeze:

- claim boundary and affected chapter candidates;
- research question and falsifiable interpretations;
- simpler baseline and all arms;
- corpus identity, splits, contamination policy, and excluded data;
- model/runtime identity and authorization;
- metrics, budgets, seeds/repeats, stopping rules, and thresholds;
- evaluator, observer, proposer, operator, and promotion roles;
- negative controls and mutation families;
- allowed `promote`, `narrow`, `no_change`, `demote`, and `refute`
  dispositions;
- public-safety, privacy, rights, and retention boundaries; and
- expected artifacts and content-addressing rules.

A pilot may debug machinery, but it must be labeled non-evidentiary and remain
preserved. Any outcome-visible change requires a versioned amendment that names
the trigger, affected fields, invalidated descendants, and why the change does
not rewrite prior results.

### Role separation

Record distinct proposer, effect observer, evaluator, and promotion-authority
interfaces even when one author operates the project. Separate implementations
must not silently share answer keys, held-out labels, or mutable result state.

This is internal methodological separation. It does not satisfy the evidence-
vector state `independence` unless the vector policy and an accepted transition
explicitly justify that change.

### Artifact and environment discipline

- Content-address every frozen input, run, checkpoint, result, and decision.
- Pin code commit, dependencies, model/tokenizer revisions, hardware/runtime,
  seeds, and relevant environment variables.
- Preserve raw public-safe observations and failed runs.
- Keep private, secret, restricted, identifying, or licensed raw material out of
  the public repository unless publication is explicitly approved.
- Make output generation deterministic where appropriate and report unavoidable
  stochasticity.
- Verify bundles from a clean checkout and a second replay process where
  feasible.
- Store large generated artifacts outside Git when appropriate, but retain
  durable hashes, manifests, retrieval authority, and honest availability state.

### Measurement discipline

- Report benefit, harm, refusal, coverage, residual, and cost together.
- Preserve per-family and per-seed outcomes; do not publish only aggregates.
- Separate calibration, validation, selection, and test use.
- Do not convert model agreement into ground truth.
- Do not convert a formal record invariant into runtime correctness.
- Do not convert a replay into transfer.
- Do not convert public availability into validity or independence.
- Accept negative or no-change outcomes without opening extra unregistered arms.

### Statistical and decision discipline

Before outcome visibility, every program must define:

- the experimental unit, unit of analysis, primary estimand, primary endpoints,
  secondary endpoints, and exact aggregation rules;
- inclusion/exclusion rules, missing-data states, failure handling, and whether
  comparisons are paired by task, seed, checkpoint, or request;
- the minimum sample/repeat rationale and the smallest effect the design can
  interpret without pretending that seeds or repeated calls are independent
  population samples;
- uncertainty reporting appropriate to the finite workload, including raw
  counts and effect sizes rather than p-values alone;
- confidence interval, bootstrap, permutation, or exact-test procedures when
  used, including random seeds and resampling unit;
- multiplicity handling for families, arms, metrics, and subgroup inspection;
- calibration/selection/test separation and a prohibition on optional stopping
  outside registered rules;
- practical decision thresholds and what result maps to each allowed
  disposition; and
- how exploratory findings are labeled and prevented from becoming
  confirmatory claims after the fact.

Do not force classical significance tests onto tiny deterministic corpora. Do
not use “three seeds” as a universal validity argument. When uncertainty cannot
support a population claim, report the finite workload exactly and narrow the
claim.

### Reproducibility and numeric portability

- Recompute every derived summary from retained atomic records.
- Separate exact byte/state identity from tolerance-based numeric equivalence.
- Specify tolerances, dtypes, accumulation order, device behavior, and accepted
  nondeterminism before comparing across hardware.
- Use fixed-order or otherwise portable accumulation when an audit metric is
  claimed exact.
- Replay from a clean checkout and fresh process; use a second operating-system
  or CPU environment when cross-platform identity is part of the claim.
- Preserve library and kernel versions and reject environment drift that can
  change decisions.
- Compare recorded summaries to recomputed outputs and include at least one
  mutation that changes a decision without changing superficial schema shape.

The v2.1 cross-CPU reduction failure is a permanent regression case for exact
numeric audit fields.

### Security, privacy, and data governance

Before admitting a corpus, model, repository, or artifact:

- record provenance, license/use terms, publication rights, retention policy,
  and whether redistribution is allowed;
- scan public-bound artifacts for secrets, credentials, personal data, private
  paths, hidden payloads, and prohibited source content;
- treat repository text, issues, tests, model output, and retrieved context as
  untrusted input capable of prompt or workflow injection;
- default experimental sandboxes to no external network and least authority;
- allow external effects only through explicitly disposable, idempotent, and
  observed adapters;
- pin and hash model weights/tokenizers without publishing restricted weights;
- preserve public-safe observable outputs without requiring hidden chain-of-
  thought;
- quarantine malicious or ambiguous artifacts before evaluator ingestion; and
- record redaction and exclusion decisions so missing artifacts are not
  represented as complete public evidence.

No program may expose credentials, private source exports, personal data, or
unsafe executable payloads merely to improve reproducibility.

### Global resource and retention budgets

Before M2, set per-program and total ceilings for:

- wall time, model calls/tokens, CPU/GPU hours, peak RAM/VRAM, and concurrency;
- disk usage for models, checkpoints, caches, raw outputs, and rendered bundles;
- network transfer and external service spend, which require explicit
  authorization if nonzero;
- retries, failed families, pilot work, and amendment reruns; and
- long-term retained bytes versus reproducible-on-demand artifacts.

Define OOM, timeout, disk-pressure, thermal, dependency-download, and runaway-
retry stop rules. A budget overrun cannot be hidden by deleting failed runs.
Large artifacts may be deduplicated or stored outside Git only after a manifest,
content digest, retrieval authority, availability state, and retention decision
exist. Cleanup must preserve the evidence needed to reproduce every reported
claim and negative result.

## Risk register

| Risk | Trigger | Required mitigation and disposition |
|---|---|---|
| Model/runtime infeasibility | No candidate passes the development-only eligibility gate within budget. | Preserve rejected runtimes, record an activation blocker, and do not substitute the v2.1 workload as a new outcome. |
| Held-out leakage | Task identity, label, answer key, or outcome enters selection, prompts, router features, or evaluator state. | Invalidate affected descendants, version the freeze, and rerun only after leakage removal. |
| Evaluator correlation or gaming | Evaluator shares labels/implementation with proposer/router or rewards a proxy that diverges from task truth. | Narrow claims, add criterion-validity probes, preserve disagreements, and prohibit promotion. |
| Incomplete effect/state inventory | An observed mutation, cache, backup, external effect, or descendant lacks an owner or rollback/deletion rule. | Forbid it, add it through an amendment, or preserve it as an out-of-scope residual; never claim completeness. |
| Compute/storage runaway | A frozen global or program budget is exceeded or repeated failures threaten disk/thermal stability. | Stop safely, preserve manifests and negative results, then amend or disposition without silent retry expansion. |
| Public-safety or rights failure | Secrets, personal/private data, restricted weights, malicious payloads, or unclear redistribution rights enter a public-bound artifact. | Quarantine, redact or exclude, record the missing-evidence boundary, and rerun public-safety validation. |
| Numeric/platform drift | Recomputed exact metrics or decisions differ across the pinned replay environments. | Preserve both observations, identify tolerance/accumulation cause, invalidate false exactness, and version any repair. |
| Public truth drift | Landing, status, release, citation, rights, product, or roadmap surfaces disagree. | Fail P0/release, fix the lower-authority surface, rebuild, redeploy, and attest. |
| Scope expansion | A new arm, chapter, source family, format, or infrastructure lane is proposed after outcome visibility. | Apply the ownership/amendment gate; defer or create a separate future roadmap when it does not belong to the frozen question. |

Risks are not closed by prose. Each triggered risk needs an evidence reference,
owner, state, and resulting invalidation or residual decision in the machine
roadmap record or program result.

## Release rule

A new release is warranted only when at least one empirical program closes with
a reproducible public-safe result and all affected dispositions are reconciled.
The result may be negative. Do not select a version merely because P0 or routine
maintenance changed prose.

Any release must bind one clean tested source state to:

- validation-registry and Lean results;
- HTML render and product projections;
- all-page/all-viewport browser evidence;
- commit-bound tested bundle;
- deployment without rebuilding;
- public status/chapter-graph attestation;
- citation and version metadata;
- exact-tag rights routing;
- release record and `/versions/` row; and
- deterministic immutable archive plus public redownload digest verification.

Optional formats require their own scope and artifact gates.
