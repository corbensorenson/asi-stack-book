## Adjudication

The chapter passes the book's distinct-interface test. Existing chapters own
specialist routing, readiness, decoding speed, mathematical/search substrates,
recurrence contracts, cyclic mechanisms, replacement, and self-improvement
governance. None owns the learned **cognitive-kernel boundary**: the contract by
which a Transformer, state-space model, recurrent cell, convolutional sequence
model, KAN component, memory-augmented controller, continuous-time network, or
future substrate can be substituted without rewriting the surrounding memory,
execution, evidence, authority, rollback, and evaluation stack.

## Core claim candidate

For an exact task family, consumer, modality, model version, memory contract,
hardware/runtime, authority and rights envelope, resource budget, evaluator,
fallback, rollback, and time horizon, a learned cognitive substrate should be a
replaceable implementation behind a typed Cognitive Kernel ABI. Admission
requires matched strong baselines, exact state and memory semantics,
proposal-versus-effect separation, evaluator independence, complete lifecycle
cost, failure and residual retention, checkpoint compatibility, and causal
ablation. Architecture names, parameter counts, asymptotic complexity, synthetic
recall, interpretability demonstrations, or source-reported benchmark wins do
not establish general superiority, safe substitution, architectural
self-improvement, or support-state movement.

## What the chapter owns

1. **Cognitive Kernel ABI.** Define substrate-neutral initialize, propose,
   receipt-consumption, checkpoint, restore, cost, capability, architecture-ID,
   state-schema, and non-claim interfaces.
2. **Route shapes above substrate.** Reflex, reaction, deliberation, search, and
   specialist heads are governed routes over the ABI, not Transformer-specific
   features. A route may choose a different kernel, combination, or deterministic
   primitive for each task without widening authority.
3. **Exact–latent separation.** Identity, values, program counters, effects,
   provenance, authority, receipts, and durable state remain exact; relevance,
   uncertainty, prediction, and heuristic proposals may be latent.
4. **Architecture portfolio.** Compare families by the computation they expose:
   global attention, recurrence, state-space compression, long convolution,
   test-time learning, external memory, continuous-time dynamics, learned
   univariate functions, graph message passing, program synthesis, and hybrids.
5. **Architectural RSI.** Recursive self-improvement includes proposing,
   training, testing, shadowing, canarying, promoting, composing, narrowing,
   rolling back, and retiring cognitive substrates—not merely updating weights
   inside a fixed architecture.
6. **KISS accounting.** Simplicity is measured over training, inference, search,
   memory, verification, routing, compilation, maintenance, migration,
   governance, fallback, and failure risk. A small core with hidden external
   burden is not automatically simpler.
7. **OneCell hypothesis.** Test whether a compact weight-tied recurrent
   microkernel plus exact memory, explicit search, world modeling, exact
   execution, and verified abstraction learning can grow capability without
   proportional central-model growth.

## Architecture taxonomy to research

The chapter must avoid a fashionable-architecture catalog. Every family is
described by state, access pattern, training parallelism, inference behavior,
hardware fit, exactness limits, adaptation surface, failure modes, and stack
interface.

| Family | Initial comparators | Questions that matter |
|---|---|---|
| Dense attention | Transformer | What broad communication and training parallelism are lost or retained? |
| Structured and selective state spaces | S4, Mamba, Mamba-2/SSD, Mamba-3, Hyperscale Lottery edge counterstudy | What information can fixed or structured state retain, where do copying/state-tracking limits appear, and do cloud-throughput gains survive edge latency, memory, and energy measurement? |
| Retention and linear recurrence | RetNet, RWKV, Gated DeltaNet | Can training parallelism coexist with stable recurrent inference, targeted overwrite/erasure, and exact state tracking? |
| Long convolution | Hyena | When do implicit long filters outperform attention or state-space baselines after kernel and hardware costs? |
| Modern gated recurrence | xLSTM and recurrent Transformers | Does shared or gated state generalize beyond trained depth/size and stop usefully? |
| Tiny recursive reasoning | TRM and controlled TRM analyses | Do small shared weights perform useful deep refinement after task identity, augmentation, sampling, voting, and compute are matched? |
| Test-time learned state | TTT layers, Titans | What may adapt online, what remains advisory, and how are contamination, rollback, and state custody handled? |
| External differentiable memory | Neural Turing Machine, Differentiable Neural Computer | Can a compact controller address variable-size exact structure reliably outside toy distributions? |
| Continuous-time dynamics | Liquid Time-Constant Networks and closed-form variants | Do irregular-time and dynamical-system advantages survive matched task and hardware comparisons? |
| Learned edge functions | KANs | Are gains specific to low-dimensional symbolic/scientific tasks, splines, initialization, or unfair parameter/FLOP accounting? |
| Graph and relational processors | Message Passing Neural Networks and neural algorithmic reasoning | Can typed relational state extrapolate in size/topology without hiding answer-bearing retrieval? |
| Program/library learning | DreamCoder, Stitch, exact interpreters | Does verified abstraction reduce held-out future search rather than rename traces? |
| Hybrid portfolios | Jamba-class attention–SSM–MoE, neural–symbolic, reflex/reaction/deliberation mixtures | Does composition improve the joint utility/cost/safety frontier or only multiply routing and verification burden? |

## Required proof and executable program

1. Define a `CognitiveKernel` contract with typed proposals, exact receipts,
   state-schema/version identity, authority request, checkpoint/restore, and cost
   reporting.
2. Implement at least three genuinely different kernels behind the same ABI:
   a strong Transformer baseline, a recurrent/state-space baseline, and one
   non-token-native or exact-search/controller lane. Deterministic oracles remain
   separately credited.
3. Prove finite interface properties: proposals do not commit effects; authority
   cannot widen through substrate replacement; checkpoint migration either
   preserves declared exact state or rejects; revoked kernels cannot route;
   fallback remains available; mixed-kernel traces preserve parentage,
   evaluator, evidence, and residual ownership.
4. Independently replay ABI conformance and reject malicious kernels that forge
   receipts, omit state, exceed budgets, hide assistance, widen authority, or
   produce incompatible checkpoints.
5. Run matched architecture tournaments under two preregistered views:
   equal-active-parameter and equal-total-cost. Also report wall time, memory,
   energy when measurable, search, verification, repair, routing, maintenance,
   and migration.
6. Use natural, algorithmic, long-context, continual, multimodal, control, and
   exact-execution tasks. Include size/depth/topology extrapolation, state
   tracking, copying, retrieval, distractors, online drift, verifier attacks,
   and hardware-sensitive workloads.
7. Test reflex, reaction, deliberation, and specialist-route heads as ABI-level
   policies across kernels. Compare single-kernel, routed, hybrid, oracle,
   random, and strong-generalist controls.
8. Run the frozen-core ratchet: hold central parameters fixed while verified
   skills and representations accumulate; measure held-out future search cost,
   transfer, false activation, lifecycle burden, and rollback locality.
9. Test architectural RSI through isolated candidate generation, training,
   evaluation, shadow, canary, promotion, rollback, and retirement. The system
   may propose a new kernel but cannot approve its own evaluator or widen its
   own authority.
10. Separate recurrent computation from addressable memory. Freeze recurrent
    state size, sparse-slot capacity, cache growth, pointer fusion, lookup cost,
    storage, invalidation, migration, and deletion as explicit experimental axes.
11. For every tiny-recursive arm, preregister canonical single-pass, matched
    sampling/voting, blank/random/unseen identity, marginal utility by recursion
    step, learned stopping, and progressive mechanism-ablation views. Do not
    credit nominal recursion depth or parameter count when useful depth or outer
    compute supplies the result.

## Falsifiers

The chapter's strong thesis narrows or fails if matched results show that:

- the ABI erases capabilities needed by competitive kernels;
- route and translation overhead dominate substrate gains;
- recurrent or state-space lanes fail critical state-tracking or extrapolation;
- KAN gains vanish under matched FLOPs/parameters or remain confined to narrow
  symbolic-function tasks;
- external or test-time memory corrupts state, cannot be rolled back, or merely
  hides context cost;
- OneCell depends on a large language adapter, privileged retrieval,
  deterministic answer-producing tools, benchmark-authored skills, or
  unreported search;
- fixed-core capability does not grow or future search cost does not decline;
- mixed architectures multiply correlated failure, evaluator capture, or
  maintenance burden;
- or a strong Transformer/hybrid baseline dominates the complete
  utility–quality–safety–rights–latency–resource frontier.

## Non-claims

- The reviewed shares do not prove OneCell, the verified-compression-ratchet
  thesis, or any post-Transformer architecture.
- No architecture named here is adopted, reproduced, or shown superior locally.
- Replaceability is not interchangeability: state, tokenizer, memory,
  checkpoint, calibration, and hardware differences require explicit adapters
  and may block substitution.
- Architectural self-improvement does not authorize autonomous deployment or
  self-approval.
