## Terminal decision

integrated as a load-bearing reference chapter at `argument` support. It owns a
distinct object and decision boundary: the maximum admissible inference and
non-authorizing governance of observations and interventions on one exact
model's internal state. Evidence States owns support movement, Benchmark
Ratchets owns behavioral instruments, Security and Weight Custody own access,
and Readiness owns release. None replaces this inference boundary.

The decision is not based on chapter count or source prestige. The chapter now
has a subject-first reader path, an exclusive-owner argument, a serious
behavior-only alternative, a minimum implementation, an exact formalization
ceiling, a non-naive claim-bearing protocol, eight bounded source mappings, a
technical diagram, failure families, non-claims, and an exact handoff. Removing
it would scatter the feature/circuit-to-policy inference ladder across adjacent
owners and make evidence-to-authority laundering harder to detect.
