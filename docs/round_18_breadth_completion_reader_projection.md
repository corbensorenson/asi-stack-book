## Opening-map delta

The reader's mental model should now distinguish four often-collapsed
transitions:

1. raw signal to admitted observation;
2. admitted observation to governed belief;
3. decision to physical effect;
4. valid individual behavior to population outcome.

It should also distinguish external alignment controls from learned-objective
integrity, and individual human oversight from institutional allocation of
authority and accountability.

Do not compress the new chapters into “more safety.” Each owns a distinct
boundary:

- perception controls what enters state;
- world modeling controls what is believed;
- inner alignment controls what learned machinery may be optimizing;
- embodiment controls what reaches the physical world;

projection even if all titles are present.
