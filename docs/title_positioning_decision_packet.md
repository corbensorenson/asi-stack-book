# Title and Positioning Decision Packet

Last updated: 2026-07-10. Decision state: selected and implemented for the
active v1.x development line.

The critique is valid: `ASI` creates a credibility burden because the current
repository is a book, evidence system, formal-record program, and bounded local
prototype—not an implemented or validated ASI. The title can still be a useful
stress-case brand, but first impressions arrive before caveats.

Four choices are recorded in `governance/title_positioning_decision.json`:
retain the current title; retain **The ASI Stack** while reframing the subtitle
around advanced AI with ASI as the stress case; rename to **The Governed AI
Stack**; or adopt the more conventional **Governed Intelligence Systems**.

The author selected the continuity/credibility compromise:
**The ASI Stack: A Governed Systems Architecture for Advanced AI, with ASI as
the Stress Case**. It preserves the v1.0 identity while changing what the title
claims the book is about.

The active manifest, generated Quarto metadata, citation file, README, landing
page, prompt, and outline move together. Historical `v1.0.0` release records
and reader artifacts keep **A Systems Architecture for Governed, Efficient,
Self-Improving AI** as the former title; they are version history, not active
title drift. The repository description and live deployment must be checked in
the same publication transaction.

Positioning does not create technical evidence, novelty, ASI capability,
safety, or readiness. The decision does not change any support state.
