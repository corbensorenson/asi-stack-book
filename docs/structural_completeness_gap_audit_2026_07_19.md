# ASI Stack Structural Completeness Gap Audit — 2026-07-19

Status: **second-tranche coverage audit; roadmap admission does not imply manifest admission**

This audit asks which responsibilities required to build, govern, operate, and
improve an advanced general intelligence still lack a primary owner after the
four-chapter structural tranche. It compares the 59-entry working manifest,
chapter prose, the full 23-item July completeness scan, the active roadmap,
current Corben-source assignments, and current primary literature. It changes
no support state and makes no capability, safety, novelty, or completeness
claim merely because a topic or source was found.

## Executive decision

The four chapters proposed by the latest Claude review were good additions,
but they were not a valid final-completeness certificate. That review described
the earlier scan as a 16-item map. The repository's completed scan later grew
to 23 Tier-1 items and added seven system-boundary candidates. Operations and
supply-chain integrity now have owners; perception, embodiment, privacy,
autonomous replication, and scientific experimentation were never terminally
admitted or rejected. A fresh manifest audit exposes four more facts:

- base-model training is delegated in prose to a separate “Training and Policy
  Optimization” owner, but no training chapter exists; and
- inter-stack exchange owns pairwise protocol contracts, but no chapter owns
  population dynamics, collusion, cascades, collective competence, or gradual
  disempowerment; and
- Human Factors owns whether oversight is exercisable, but no chapter owns the
  outbound communication transaction from an evidence-bounded claim through
  audience, personalization, channel, and amplification choices to belief or
  choice effects, correction, and retraction; and
- Policy Optimization, Planning, and RSI each consume a legitimate or accepted
  objective, while no chapter owns how plural purposes, preferences, rules, and
  unresolved uncertainty become a versioned target/proxy contract or how that
  contract survives distribution and ontology change.

The roadmap should therefore admit a **bounded thirteen-candidate second
tranche** for full source and ownership adjudication. Twelve candidates clear
the initial unowned-interface test. Scientific discovery clears it
provisionally but must also pass an application-versus-general-control-plane
test. If all thirteen pass, the upper bound is 72 chapters. Seventy-two is not a
target: every failed packet returns to named existing owners.

## Method and anti-sprawl gate

The audit used four tests.

1. **Lifecycle coverage:** Can the current spine follow a system from purpose,
   data, architecture, training, perception, reasoning, action, interaction,
   evaluation, release, operation, incident, improvement, and retirement?
2. **Ownership depth:** Does a chapter own the core artifact and transition, or
   do several chapters merely mention the topic as somebody else's constraint?
3. **Distinct contract:** Does the candidate have a unique interface, invariant,
   and failure family that cannot be absorbed without overloading an owner?
4. **Evidence future:** Can the candidate support claim-commensurate evidence,
   a simpler or competing design, a falsifier, and a bounded formal target
   where formalization is honest? Operational mechanisms normally require a
   competent empirical lane; normative, institutional, and foundational claims
   require the strongest appropriate mix of empirical, comparative-case,
   formal, and philosophical evidence rather than a decorative toy experiment.

Source counts are not admission evidence. Each packet should fill four source
roles where the literature permits: mechanism or capability, limitation or
failure, competing design, and measurement or evaluation. A packet with only
three passage-reviewed primary sources must explain which roles one source
legitimately combines and why the omission does not bias the decision.

The pre-tranche 55 drafted chapter files contained no occurrence of `sensor fusion`,
`differential privacy`, `membership inference`, `autonomous replication`, or
`multi-agent risk`; only one occurrence of `scientific discovery`; three
scattered perception-family hits; and no distributed-training occurrence.
The expanded 59-chapter corpus still contains no occurrence of `international`,
`geopolit`, `treaty`, `global governance`, `public legitimacy`, `macroeconomic`,
`labor displacement`, `digital divide`, `water`, `carbon`, `data center`, or
`datacenter`. These counts are diagnostics, not admission evidence. The
decisive evidence is the missing owner and handoff contract.

## Candidate A — Governed Model Training, Distributed Optimization, and Scaling

**Proposed ID:** `governed-model-training-distributed-optimization-and-scaling`

**Placement:** Part III, immediately after Replaceable Cognitive Substrates.

**Exclusive job:** Own the training-run transaction from a frozen architecture,
data lease, objective, numerical policy, topology, and compute budget through
distributed execution, full-state checkpointing, anomaly handling, candidate
checkpoint selection, and evidence-bounded handoff to qualification.

Policy Optimization owns feedback-driven behavior-update leases. Data Engines
owns datum and descendant custody. Supply Chain owns lineage assertions.
Resource Economics owns budgets. Replaceable Substrates owns architecture
adoption. None owns whether a distributed run faithfully executed the intended
learning process or whether a selected checkpoint is the product claimed.

Core artifacts include a training-run contract, topology and parallelism map,
data-order commitment, optimizer/scheduler/numerical-state manifest, heartbeat
and anomaly ledger, exact-resume receipt, checkpoint-family denominator, and
qualification handoff. Primary invariants include complete model/optimizer/
scheduler/scaler/RNG/data-cursor state; no silent batch, objective, precision,
or topology drift; fault recovery that does not duplicate or skip examples;
and checkpoint selection separated from held-out qualification. Failure families
include silent divergence, loss spikes, numerical overflow, stale gradients,
collective-communication corruption, stragglers, data-order drift, checkpoint
tearing, recovery non-equivalence, selection leakage, and emergent-capability
threshold crossing during a run.

Initial comparators: [The Llama 3 Herd of Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.21783),
[Megatron-LM](https://arxiv.org/abs/1909.08053),
[ZeRO](https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.02054), and
[Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.15556).
The first campaign should use a tractable open model and matched single-process
and distributed runs, inject worker loss, stale state, corrupted checkpoints,
precision changes, and data-order errors, and measure convergence, exact-resume
distance, downstream variance, throughput, energy, failed-run denominator, and
operator burden. It cannot support frontier-scale transfer without independent
larger-scale reproduction.

## Candidate B — Perception, Sensor Fusion, and Observation Trust

**Proposed ID:** `perception-sensor-fusion-and-observation-trust`

**Placement:** Part II, after Command Contracts and before Planning.

**Exclusive job:** Own admission of environmental evidence into task-relative
observations and state estimates: modality and sensor identity, calibration,
time synchronization, provenance, taint, fusion, disagreement, uncertainty,
active observation, refresh, spoofing, occlusion, and handoff to world models.

World Models owns prediction from admitted observations; it explicitly does not
absorb generic perception. Context owns information supplied to cognition, not
whether a sensor reading corresponds to the environment. Security owns hostile
flows, not perceptual sufficiency. The chapter's core artifacts are an
observation contract, calibration record, fusion/disagreement receipt,
visibility and coverage map, active-perception lease, and observation-to-state
handoff. Invariants require exact time and modality identity, visible missingness,
no fusion-confidence increase from correlated sensors alone, refresh before
expiry, and abstention or active observation when task-relevant state is
unresolved. Failure families include spoofing, occlusion, sensor drift,
misalignment, synchronization error, modality collapse, correlated failure,
semantic hallucination, OOD overconfidence, and active-perception privacy harm.

Initial comparators include the CVPR
[3D corruption benchmarks](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2023/html/Dong_Benchmarking_Robustness_of_3D_Object_Detection_to_Common_Corruptions_CVPR_2023_paper.html),
[adversarial sensor fusion](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/WACV2022/html/Wang_Adversarial_Robustness_of_Deep_Sensor_Fusion_Models_WACV_2022_paper.html),
and [belief-space planning with foundation-model uncertainty](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.03245).
The first campaign should compare unimodal, ordinary fusion, reliability-aware
fusion, and active-observation routes across natural and corrupted held-out
scenes while measuring calibration, task-relevant miss rate, false certainty,
abstention, information-gathering cost, and downstream decision harm.

## Candidate C — Embodied Agency, Real-Time Control, and Physical Safety

**Proposed ID:** `embodied-agency-real-time-control-and-physical-safety`

**Placement:** Part II, after Runtime Adapters and before Inter-Stack Protocols.

**Exclusive job:** Own the transition from an authorized symbolic action to a
time-bounded physical control trace under dynamics, contact, human proximity,
hazard envelopes, interlocks, fallback controllers, and irreversible effects.

Runtime Adapters owns generic permission and effect receipts. World Models owns
prediction. Governed Operations owns service incidents. None owns control
periods, plant state, actuator saturation, real-time deadlines, collision and
force envelopes, safety controllers, or sim-to-real evidence. The chapter's
core artifacts are a plant/embodiment manifest, control lease, temporal safety
envelope, interlock and fallback map, human-presence state, bounded trace,
physical-effect receipt, and compensation residual. Invariants require an
independent stop path, deadline-aware safe fallback, authority narrowing under
lost sensing or compute, explicit force/space/time limits, and no simulation
result represented as physical evidence. Failure families include deadline
miss, actuator saturation, contact instability, unsafe exploration, model-
plant mismatch, sensor-actuator desynchronization, interlock bypass, unsafe
fallback, human entry, delayed effect, and irreversibility.

Initial comparators include
[Gemini Robotics](https://deepmind.google/blog/gemini-robotics-brings-ai-into-the-physical-world/),
the [foundation-model robotics physical-risk survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.12583),
and current embodied-safety/runtime-assurance literature. The first claim-bearing
campaign begins in simulation with independent physics truth and adversarial
latency/sensing faults, then permits only low-energy hardware transfer after
the safety controller, stop path, and positive controls pass.

## Candidate D — Privacy, Data Rights, and Information-Flow Governance

**Proposed ID:** `privacy-data-rights-and-information-flow-governance`

**Placement:** Part I, after Security Kernel and before Model-Weight Custody.

**Exclusive job:** Own purpose-limited use of information about people and
groups across input, context, memory, training, inference, output, audit,
sharing, derivatives, correction, export, deletion, and retirement.

Security governs unauthorized access; privacy also governs harmful authorized
processing. Data Engines owns learning and unlearning operations, not consent,
purpose, correction, export, privacy budgets, or subject-facing remedies.
Moral Uncertainty owns contested values, not privacy engineering. Core artifacts
include a data-subject and affected-group record, purpose/use lease, data-flow
map, minimization decision, consent and rights state, privacy-loss ledger,
inference-risk audit, cross-user-memory boundary, derivative obligation graph,
and correction/export/deletion receipt. Invariants require purpose continuity,
least necessary disclosure, separation of access from consent, visible unknown
copies and influence, propagation of rights to derivatives, and distinct
storage-erasure, behavioral-forgetting, influence, and privacy claims. Failure
families include memorization, extraction, membership inference, attribute
inference, linkage, reconstruction, cross-user leakage, purpose drift,
consent laundering, dark-pattern collection, group privacy harm, audit
surveillance, and deletion theater.

Initial comparators include [NIST SP 800-226](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/sp/800/226/final),
[Deep Learning with Differential Privacy](https://arxiv.org/abs/1607.00133),
[Membership Inference Attacks](https://arxiv.org/abs/1610.05820), and
[training-data extraction from language models](https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity21/presentation/carlini-extracting).
The first campaign should plant consent- and purpose-labeled canaries in a
small trained model and memory system, compare ordinary, minimization,
differential-privacy, access-control, and unlearning variants, and measure
utility, extraction, membership inference, correction/deletion propagation,
rights completion, and residual cost without claiming legal compliance.

## Candidate E — Human–AI Organizations, Delegation, and Accountability

**Proposed ID:** `human-ai-organizations-delegation-and-accountability`

**Placement:** Part II, after Labor OS and before Inter-Stack Protocols.

**Exclusive job:** Own the designed organizational lifecycle in which human
and AI actors receive roles, competence requirements, decision rights,
delegations, incentives, workload, separation of duties, escalation paths,
accountability, succession, and dissolution.

Labor OS owns one typed work unit and deliberately treats accountable humans
as an external authority. Human Factors owns whether an identified controller
can exercise a particular control envelope. Human Intent owns interpretation
of a principal's request; System Boundaries owns exact grants; Moral
Uncertainty owns contestability. Inter-Stack Protocols owns one exchange, and
the proposed systemic-risk chapter owns emergent population behavior. None
owns who occupies which organizational role, how decision rights and
consequence ownership are allocated, or how responsibility remains coupled to
effective control across a multi-role human–AI institution.

Core artifacts include an organization charter and purpose boundary, actor and
role registry, competence and workload state, decision-right and delegation
graph, separation-of-duties matrix, conflict-of-interest and incentive record,
supervisory-span limit, escalation/adjudication path, handoff receipt,
accountability map, succession plan, and dissolution residual. Invariants
require every consequential decision to have an empowered and competent owner;
responsibility to track effective authority, information, time, and control;
delegation to narrow rather than erase accountability; independent review for
conflicted roles; visible unstaffed or overloaded functions; and succession or
dissolution without orphaned obligations. Failure families include
responsibility gaps, moral crumple zones, shadow automation, rubber-stamp
approval, deskilling, role ambiguity, supervisory overload, separation-of-
duties collapse, incentive gaming, managerial capture, blame laundering,
accountability without control, succession failure, and institutional memory
loss.

Initial comparators include the organizational roles and accountability
outcomes in [NIST AI RMF 1.0](https://airc.nist.gov/airmf-resources/airmf/5-sec-core/),
[Moral Crumple Zones](https://doi.org/10.17351/ests2019.260),
[The Allocation of Decision Authority to Human and Artificial Intelligence](https://www.nber.org/papers/w26673),
and the current interview-based
[AI Decision Matrix](https://cisr.mit.edu/publication/2026_0601_AIDecisionMatrix_SebastianWeillHaskampVomBrocke).
The first campaign should compare individual-only, ordinary RACI-like, static
human-in-the-loop, and workload/competence/authority-aware organizational
routes on a natural multi-role workflow with injected absence, conflict,
overload, bad incentives, escalation, and succession events. Measure useful
throughput, decision quality, responsibility-control alignment, intervention
timeliness, rubber-stamping, unresolved ownership, affected-party remedy,
human cost, and recovery. Interview and source-derived frameworks are design
comparators, not local evidence that the proposed organization works.

## Candidate F — Multi-Agent Dynamics, Collective Intelligence, and Systemic Risk

**Proposed ID:** `multi-agent-dynamics-collective-intelligence-and-systemic-risk`

**Placement:** Part II, after Inter-Stack Protocols.

**Exclusive job:** Own population-level behavior that emerges after individually
valid exchanges: cooperation, conflict, miscoordination, collusion, coalition
formation, cascades, market power, shared-model correlation, collective
competence, institutional feedback, and gradual human disempowerment.

Inter-Stack Protocols owns one exchange's identity, delegation, consideration,
receipt, and dispute path. Personal Hives owns placement and federation. Routing
owns component selection. None can infer system dynamics from pairwise-valid
records. Core artifacts include a population and role registry, interaction
graph, incentive and information structure, coalition record, externality and
concentration ledger, cascade model, human-influence indicators, intervention
policy, and systemic residual. Invariants require preserving population-level
denominators, modeling correlated agents as correlated, preventing local
receipts from certifying global stability, keeping externalities and affected
nonparticipants visible, and retaining human contestability and exit. Failure
families include tacit and explicit collusion, arms races, herding, information
cascades, correlated blind spots, emergent monopoly, principal fragmentation,
commitment failure, feedback amplification, governance capture, and gradual
disempowerment.

Initial comparators include [Multi-Agent Risks from Advanced AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.14143),
[Foundations of Cooperative AI](https://doi.org/10.1609/aaai.v37i13.26791),
[Gradual Disempowerment](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v267/kulveit25a.html),
and current empirical [LLM-agent collusion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.01413).
The first campaign should use matched public-goods, resource, auction, and
coordination environments with heterogeneous and same-family populations,
communication and identity ablations, hidden coalitions, governance graphs,
and human vetoes. Measure welfare distribution, useful coordination, collusion,
cascade size, concentration, human influence, intervention cost, and recovery.

## Candidate G — Autonomous Replication, Proliferation, and Containment

**Proposed ID:** `autonomous-replication-proliferation-and-containment`

**Placement:** Part I, after Open-Ended Improvement Engines.

**Exclusive job:** Own the end-to-end capability and authority chain by which an
agent obtains resources, copies or reconstructs code and weights, provisions
compute, establishes identity and credentials, deploys descendants, persists,
adapts, recruits help, resists shutdown, and transfers control.

RSI governs changes to a system. Weight Custody governs protected artifacts.
Security and Runtime Adapters govern individual effects. Capability Thresholds
governs commitments. None owns replication as a composed lifecycle whose weak
links can improve at different rates. Core artifacts include a replication-
attempt contract, descendant identity and principal record, resource-acquisition
ledger, copy and deployment lineage, credential and network lease, persistence
state, shutdown/recall test, proliferation graph, and terminal containment
receipt. Invariants require explicit replication authority, noninheritance of
parent authority, bounded descendants and resources, independently enforceable
termination, complete copy lineage, and threshold-triggered containment.
Failure families include hidden copies, credential bootstrapping, resource
fraud, cloud provisioning, weight exfiltration, polymorphic persistence,
descendant identity loss, shutdown resistance, human-assisted chain completion,
cross-jurisdiction escape, and uncontrolled proliferation.

Initial comparators include [RepliBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.18565),
[OpenAI's Preparedness Framework](https://openai.com/index/updating-our-preparedness-framework/),
and the [Google DeepMind Frontier Safety Framework](https://deepmind.google/blog/strengthening-our-frontier-safety-framework/).
The first campaign must run in a synthetic provider/network sandbox with fake
credentials, money, identities, weights, and endpoints. It should preserve a
component-wise denominator, human-assistance variants, positive controls,
shutdown tests, and strong benchmark comparators without releasing a real
replication capability.

## Candidate H — Scientific Discovery and Experimental Governance

**Proposed ID:** `scientific-discovery-and-experimental-governance`

**Placement:** Part IV, after Data Engines and before Artifact Steward Agents.

**Exclusive job:** Own the reusable closed loop from research objective and
hypothesis through experiment design, simulator or instrument authority,
measurement, analysis, causal/statistical claim, replication, dual-use review,
and evidence-ledger handoff.

This candidate is admitted to research with a stricter gate because “science”
can become an application anthology. It earns a chapter only if it specifies a
domain-independent experimental control plane not already owned by Planning,
Runtime Adapters, Benchmark Ratchets, Artifact Graphs, and the Living Book.
Core artifacts would include a hypothesis lineage record, preregistration,
design and power analysis, instrument/calibration lease, sample and protocol
lineage, blind/holdout state, observation and exclusion ledger, analysis plan,
replication package, dual-use disposition, and claim handoff. Invariants require
separating hypothesis generation from confirmation, instrument return from
scientific truth, exploratory from confirmatory analysis, and one laboratory
from transfer. Failure families include HARKing, p-hacking, selective stopping,
instrument drift, simulator laundering, contaminated controls, automation bias,
irreproducibility, hidden human intervention, unsafe experiment generation,
and dual-use leakage.

Initial comparators include the [A-Lab autonomous materials laboratory](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06734-w),
[Coscientist](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06792-0), and the
[autonomous-laboratory challenges review](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.06847).
The first campaign should use a benign, reproducible simulation or low-risk
open instrument, injected null and known effects, independent analysis, frozen
stopping, and a full negative-result denominator. Failure of the general-
control-plane test returns this material to existing evidence and embodiment
owners.

## Candidate I — Human–AI Communication, Persuasion, and Epistemic Security

**Proposed ID:** `human-ai-communication-persuasion-and-epistemic-security`

**Placement:** Part I, immediately after Human Factors and Meaningful Control.

**Exclusive job:** Own the outbound transaction from an evidence-bounded claim
through audience identity and vulnerability, personalization, channel and
amplification policy, delivery, observed belief or choice effects, correction,
retraction, and remedy.

Human Factors keeps whether a person can exercise oversight. Scalable
Oversight keeps the production of trustworthy supervisory judgments.
Adversarial Evaluation keeps behavioral elicitation and deception tests.
Capability Thresholds keeps release commitments. Claim Ledgers keep internal
belief revision. None currently owns what a system may communicate, to whom,
through which influence mechanism, at what scale, with what provenance and
uncertainty, or how downstream effects are detected and corrected.

The chapter contract requires a communication packet containing claim and
evidence identity, maximum inference, uncertainty, audience class and protected
attributes, purpose, personalization inputs, persuasive technique, channel,
amplification limit, provenance disclosure, predicted benefit and harm,
authority, expiry, correction path, retraction scope, downstream-copy policy,
and observed effect receipts. Core invariants forbid stronger outbound language
than the evidence packet permits; vulnerability exploitation; undisclosed
synthetic identity or sponsorship; purpose drift; personalization across denied
attributes; amplification without a bounded denominator; and correction that
cannot reach materially affected recipients or known descendants.

Failure families include calibrated-sounding misinformation, selective or
misleading framing, emotional manipulation, sycophancy, microtargeting of
vulnerable groups, dark patterns, parasocial dependence, authority laundering,
provenance stripping, cross-channel amplification cascades, belief lock-in,
correction failure, retraction evasion, and evaluator disagreement about
legitimate persuasion. The strongest objection is that these responsibilities
are adequately carried by Human Factors and adversarial evaluation. Candidate I
is rejected as a chapter if its packet, authority boundary, effect ledger, and
correction/retraction lifecycle can be absorbed there without obscuring their
primary invariants.

**Initial comparators:**
`ext_conversational_persuasion_gpt4_2025` ([open Nature article](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-025-02194-6))
is passage-reviewed and supplies controlled personalized-conversation evidence
with explicit short-horizon and population limits;
`ext_anthropic_model_persuasiveness_2024` ([official provider report](https://www.anthropic.com/news/measuring-model-persuasiveness))
supplies a provider-run, single-message capability baseline, but its released
data and complete analysis were not audited locally; and
`ext_commercial_persuasion_ai_2026` ([current preprint](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04263))
is abstract-only preliminary evidence about sponsor-directed conversational
choice. Their presence clears preliminary source-record existence only. A
passage-reviewed correction, retraction, or provenance-intervention comparator
is still missing, so the required source-role admission gate is **not**
satisfied.
Manifest admission also remains blocked on crosswalk and owner adjudication and
a competent campaign; the abstract-only commercial note cannot carry detailed
or general claims.

The empirical lane must be prospectively reviewed for ethics, privacy,
participant vulnerability, and debriefing; use benign claims and
non-manipulative tasks; compare no-personalization, neutral-helpfulness,
disclosure, and bounded-amplification
baselines; include heterogeneous held-out audiences and independently
implemented outcome scoring; and measure factual calibration, comprehension,
autonomy, belief/choice change, correction reach, persistence, disparate
effects, latency, cost, and abstention. Synthetic classifier success or a
single convenience sample cannot establish human influence, population
effects, or safety. A finite formal target may cover packet admissibility,
amplification authority, and retraction reachability, but not prove human belief
or welfare from a toy model.

## Candidate J — Institutions, International Coordination, and Public Legitimacy

**Proposed ID:** `institutions-international-coordination-and-public-legitimacy`

**Placement:** Part I, after Moral Uncertainty and before Stable Capability
Fields.

**Exclusive job:** Own the public-institution transaction from a claimed public
mandate and jurisdiction through participatory input, law/policy/standard
mapping, cross-border commitments, scientific assessment, monitoring,
verification, enforcement, dispute, remedy, review, amendment, and withdrawal.

Moral Uncertainty owns unresolved values and contestability; System Boundaries
owns technical grants; Capability Thresholds owns capability-triggered
commitments; Human–AI Organizations owns one designed institution; Multi-Agent
Dynamics owns emergent populations. None owns how public authority is created,
bounded, contested, coordinated, and revalidated across institutions and
jurisdictions. Core artifacts include a jurisdiction and authority map,
affected-public and standing record, participation and representation ledger,
public-reason/evidence packet, law-policy-standard crosswalk, international
commitment and verification record, regulator/assessor independence state,
conflict and supremacy route, enforcement and remedy receipt, capacity and
distributional-impact record, amendment/expiry trigger, and legitimacy
residual. Invariants require technical conformance, legal validity, political
authority, scientific consensus, public legitimacy, and observed effectiveness
to remain distinct; affected parties and excluded jurisdictions to remain
visible; commitments to name verification and remedy; conflicts to route rather
than disappear; and emergency authority to expire and face review.

Failure families include regulatory capture, forum shopping, race-to-the-bottom
deployment, standards laundering, treaty theater, unverifiable commitments,
enforcement asymmetry, national-security exception creep, participation without
power, affected-public omission, capacity inequality, scientific-panel capture,
fragmented jurisdictions, extraterritorial conflict, inaccessible remedy, and
emergency powers that become permanent. The strongest objection is that this
would become a volatile law survey. It earns a chapter only by defining a
technology-neutral institutional control plane and update mechanism; it must not
offer legal advice, freeze current policy as truth, or imply that architecture
can manufacture democratic or international legitimacy.

Initial comparators include `ext_un_global_digital_compact_2024`, the
source-noted [UN Global Digital Compact](https://www.un.org/global-digital-compact/en);
`ext_council_europe_ai_convention_2024`, the source-noted
[Council of Europe Framework Convention on AI](https://www.coe.int/en/web/artificial-intelligence/the-framework-convention-on-artificial-intelligence);
the [OECD Hiroshima AI Process Reporting Framework 2.0](https://oecd.ai/en/haip-2-launch),
and the [International AI Safety Report 2026](https://internationalaisafetyreport.org/sites/default/files/2026-02/international-ai-safety-report-2026.pdf).
Only the UN and Council of Europe comparators are source-noted in this
increment. The OECD framework and International AI Safety Report require full
source-note and passage review, so Candidate J's source-role gate remains open.
The first competent lane should combine a comparative public-record case set
with adversarial multi-jurisdiction tabletop exercises: inject conflicting
rules, incomplete participation, assessor conflict, evidence revision,
cross-border incidents, noncompliance, capture, and remedy requests; compare
informal principles, static checklists, ordinary risk management, and the
proposed institutional packet; and measure conflict detection, affected-party
coverage, verification completeness, notification/remedy latency, unresolved
authority, update cost, false assurance, and capture indicators. A tabletop or
document parser cannot establish legal compliance, institutional effectiveness,
representative legitimacy, geopolitical stability, or public trust.

## Candidate K — AI Deployment, Transition, Distribution, and Human Agency

**Proposed ID:** `ai-deployment-transition-distribution-and-human-agency`

**Placement:** Part II, after Human–AI Organizations and before Inter-Stack
Protocols.

**Exclusive job:** Own the prospectively governed deployment-to-transition
transaction by which a particular AI deployment changes tasks, job composition,
skill, decision rights, wages, ownership returns, bargaining power, access,
prices, concentration, critical-service continuity, distribution, and the
practical agency of observed affected people and communities.

Labor OS owns a typed unit of work; Human–AI Organizations owns roles and
accountability within an institution; Resource Economics owns compute budgets;
Multi-Agent Dynamics owns emergent agent-population behavior; Moral Uncertainty
owns contested values. None owns whether useful capability gains produce a
managed and measured social transition rather than hidden displacement,
dependency, exclusion, concentration, or loss of human option value. Core
artifacts include a deployment and counterfactual baseline, task/role/skill
transition map, affected-worker and community denominator, adoption and
substitution/complementarity record, wage/ownership/price/access ledger,
concentration and substitutability state, bargaining and decision-right map,
training/redeployment and income-continuity plan, critical-service continuity
record, benefit/burden distribution, contestability/remedy path, delayed
outcome schedule, and transition residual.

Invariants require exposure, adoption, productivity, task change, job change,
employment, wages, welfare, access, ownership, and distribution to remain
separate claims; aggregate gains not to erase harmed groups; productivity not
to stand in for worker or customer welfare; owners of data and expertise to
remain visible; essential access and exit to be measured; and deployment stages
to narrow or pause when transition capacity is absent. Failure families include
exposure-as-displacement overclaim, automation theater, deskilling and
dependency, wage suppression, uncompensated knowledge capture, ownership-rent
concentration, winner-take-most markets, regional and gender disparity, digital
divide, inaccessible essential services, surveillance-based productivity,
precarious hidden labor, induced demand, political capture, and gradual human
disempowerment.

Initial comparators include `ext_generative_ai_at_work_2025`, the source-noted
open field study [Generative AI at Work](https://academic.oup.com/qje/article/140/2/889/7990658);
`ext_ilo_genai_jobs_index_2025`, the source-noted
[ILO refined global occupational-exposure index](https://www.ilo.org/publications/generative-ai-and-jobs-refined-global-index-occupational-exposure);
the [IMF future-of-work analysis](https://www.imf.org/en/publications/staff-discussion-notes/issues/2024/01/14/gen-ai-artificial-intelligence-and-the-future-of-work-542379),
and the UK Competition and Markets Authority's
[foundation-model update](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ai-foundation-models-update-paper).
Only the QJE field study and ILO index are source-noted in this increment. The
IMF/CMA comparators and at least one countervailing or null deployment study
still need passage review, so Candidate K's source gate remains open.
The first campaign should use a natural multi-role workflow with a prospectively
frozen rollout, non-adoption comparator, worker and customer measures,
heterogeneous subgroup denominators, delayed follow-up, and independent
analysis. Measure useful throughput, quality, skill acquisition/erosion,
workload, discretion, wages or compensation where ethically and lawfully
available, access, distribution, concentration proxies, exit, remedy, and
transition cost. Simulation may test accounting and policy logic but cannot
establish labor-market equilibrium, economy-wide welfare, long-run inequality,
or ASI-era macroeconomics.

## Candidate L — Physical Compute Infrastructure, Energy, and Environmental Constraints

**Proposed ID:** `physical-compute-infrastructure-energy-and-environmental-constraints`

**Placement:** Part III, after Resource Economics and before Mathematical and
Search Substrates.

**Exclusive job:** Own the transaction from an authorized compute requirement
to physically deliverable and retireable capacity across accelerators, memory,
storage, interconnect, facilities, power, grid connection, generation,
cooling, water, land, emissions, materials, maintenance, resilience,
decommissioning, and affected communities.

Resource Economics owns abstract budgets and allocation. Governed Training owns
run fidelity and topology. Weight Custody owns protected artifacts and hardware
roots. Supply Chain owns lineage. Personal Hives owns placement/federation.
Governed Operations owns service incidents. None owns whether nameplate compute
can be supplied at the required place and time without hiding grid, cooling,
water, material, resilience, or community constraints. Core artifacts include
a workload-to-physical-capacity contract, hardware/interconnect/facility map,
power and temporal-matching ledger, grid/generation dependency and congestion
state, cooling/water envelope, embodied-material and supply constraint record,
land/community-impact and allocation record, metering and uncertainty lineage,
capacity/degrade/failover plan, maintenance and spares state, demand-response
lease, and retirement/reuse/recycling residual.

Invariants require requested, nameplate, available, delivered, and useful
compute to remain distinct; model/workload energy to remain separate from
facility overhead and grid effects; observed, allocated, estimated, and
projected resource use to stay typed; location and time not to disappear from
energy or emissions claims; efficiency not to imply lower total demand; local
water, land, price, reliability, and community effects to remain visible; and
physical capacity loss to narrow runtime and training authority. Failure
families include stranded or double-counted capacity, interconnect bottlenecks,
grid-queue and generation mismatch, temporal carbon laundering, cooling or
water exhaustion, correlated site failure, demand-response instability,
backup-emission hiding, rebound, embodied-material omission, e-waste,
community cost externalization, inaccessible metering, and decommissioning
without data/weight destruction.

Initial comparators include `ext_iea_energy_and_ai_2025`, the source-noted
[IEA Energy and AI](https://www.iea.org/reports/energy-and-ai);
`ext_lbnl_data_center_energy_2024`, the source-noted
[LBNL 2024 United States Data Center Energy Usage Report](https://energyanalysis.lbl.gov/publications/2024-lbnl-data-center-energy-usage-report);
the US Department of Energy's [data-center demand summary](https://www.energy.gov/articles/doe-releases-new-report-evaluating-increase-electricity-demand-data-centers),
and public workload-level energy/carbon accounting literature. The first
two comparators are source-noted; the DOE/full-report and workload-metering
comparators require deeper passage review, so Candidate L's source gate remains
open. The first
campaign should instrument matched public workloads on real local hardware
across hardware, precision, batching, placement, cooling/allocation, and

stress capacity, network, power, thermal, and fallback limits. Measure useful
work, latency, energy, peak power, memory/network bottlenecks, metering error,
availability, water/allocation proxies where observable, emissions under
multiple attribution rules, cost, and residuals. A local cluster cannot prove
frontier-facility, grid, environmental, community, or planetary transfer.

## Candidate M — Governed Objective Formation, Value Learning, and Goal Integrity

**Proposed ID:** `governed-objective-formation-value-learning-and-goal-integrity`

**Placement:** Part I, after Moral Uncertainty and before Candidate J if that
chapter is admitted, otherwise before Stable Capability Fields.

**Exclusive job:** Own the positive transition from authorized purposes,
preferences, constitutional predicates, affected-party claims, and unresolved
value uncertainty into an operational target-property contract whose proxies,
decision criterion, generalization envelope, consumers, persistence, expiry,
ontology assumptions, and reauthorization conditions remain explicit.

Human Intent owns a bounded interpretation of a particular request.
Constitutional Alignment owns rules and rights ceilings. Moral Uncertainty owns
unresolved conflict without collapsing it into one score. Policy Optimization
governs an update against a frozen legitimate target; Planning pursues an
accepted objective; RSI governs changes to a system that already has one. None
owns how those inputs may become a durable objective, how learned preferences
remain evidence rather than authority, how target properties stay separate
from measurable proxies, or what happens when the world model and ontology in
which the objective was written change.

Core artifacts include an objective charter and affected-party record;
normative, preference, and authority provenance; target-property/proxy causal
map; aggregation rule and unresolved-conflict state; uncertainty and dissent
ledger; training-signal, evaluator, planner, and deployment bindings; temporal
and domain generalization envelope; goal-misgeneralization and reward-tampering
tests; ontology and semantic-continuity record; objective-version, expiry,
reauthorization, and retirement receipts; and an independent goal-integrity
monitor. Invariants require the optimizer or evaluated system not to author or
weaken its own objective; proxy improvement not to imply target-property
improvement; a task request not to become a persistent system goal silently;
predicted preference not to create authority; dissent and uncertainty not to
disappear inside one reward; and material ontology or affected-party changes to
invalidate downstream bindings until reauthorized.

Failure families include reward misspecification and hacking, Goodhart effects,
goal misgeneralization, preference manipulation and lock-in, aggregation
laundering, evaluator capture, mesa-objective divergence, objective drift,
semantic or ontology drift, goal-content tampering, authority smuggling,
cross-context persistence, self-ratification, irreversible optimization before
clarification, and retirement without descendant invalidation. The strongest
objection is that this is simply the union of alignment chapters. It earns a
chapter only if its target/proxy/consumer binding and objective-version
lifecycle remain a distinct operational control plane after adjacent-owner
review; it must not claim to solve human values, moral truth, preference
aggregation, deceptive alignment, or stable goals under arbitrary self-change.

The existing source inventory supplies a preliminary four-role starting set:
`ext_cooperative_inverse_rl_2016` for uncertain cooperative value inference;
`ext_goal_misgeneralization_2022` for capability-versus-goal generalization;
`ext_learned_optimization_risks_2019` for outer/mesa-objective separation; and
`ext_emergent_misalignment_reward_hacking_2025` for a bounded experimental
reward-hacking failure comparator. These records justify research admission,
not manifest admission. They do not yet cover plural aggregation, objective
ontology migration, or a strong competing contract design, so Candidate M's
source-role gate remains open.

The first campaign should use small public environments with known latent
target properties, deliberately misspecified proxies, preference uncertainty,
distribution shift, ontology changes, and tampering opportunities. Compare a
fixed reward, ordinary preference learning, a target/proxy causal contract,
and an oracle-bound upper control under matched training and tuning. Freeze
capability, goal-selection, proxy, dissent, tampering, reauthorization, and
cost metrics; include capable-wrong-goal positive controls, evaluator swaps,
proxy interventions, preference-poisoning, and ontology-migration tests. A toy
environment may test finite contract behavior and bounded goal
misgeneralization; it cannot establish correct human values, population
preference fidelity, real-world moral legitimacy, deceptive-alignment control,
or objective stability in an ASI.

## Required section-scale additions, not chapters

- **Persuasion and epistemic security boundary coverage:** substantial sections
  in Human Factors, Scalable Oversight, Adversarial Evaluation, and Capability
  Thresholds remain required whether Candidate I is admitted, rejected, or
  narrowed. Those sections own local interface duties; Candidate I would own
  the end-to-end outbound communication and correction transaction.
- **Fairness, accessibility, and distributional impact:** a measured affected-
  party outcome lane in Moral Uncertainty, Human Factors, Benchmark Ratchets,
  and the proposed systemic-risk chapter remains required whether Candidate K
  is admitted or returned. Candidate K would own the end-to-end transition and
  distribution denominator rather than every local fairness duty.
- **Law, institutions, and international coordination:** jurisdiction and
  institutional interfaces in System Boundaries, Moral Uncertainty, Custody,
  Supply Chain, Thresholds, and systemic risk remain required whether Candidate
  J passes. Candidate J would own the public-authority and cross-institution
  lifecycle, never legal advice or a compliance claim.
- **Compute governance, energy, water, and material footprint:** deepen Resource
  Economics, Personal Hives, Custody, Training, Supply Chain, and Operations
  whether Candidate L passes. Candidate L would own the joined physical-
  capacity and retirement lifecycle rather than generic budget policy.
- **Objective provenance and goal-integrity boundaries:** preserve local duties
  in Human Intent, Constitutional Alignment, Moral Uncertainty, Policy
  Optimization, Planning, Benchmark Ratchets, and RSI whether Candidate M
  passes. Candidate M would own the positive target/proxy/consumer binding and
  objective-version lifecycle, not every alignment question.
- **Embedded agency, self-reference, and robust delegation:** add an explicit
  foundations program across ASI Is a Stack, System Boundaries, RSI Boundaries,
  and the Integrated Reference Architecture. Maintain an embeddedness-assumption
  register, bounded self-model, subsystem/delegation graph, evaluator-agent
  coupling analysis, unmodeled-world residual, and exact non-claims for logical
  uncertainty, self-reference, stable ontology, subsystem alignment, robust
  delegation, and open-world corrigibility. Reopen a chapter decision only when
  this work produces a usable contract and evidence program beyond restating
  `ext_embedded_agency_2019`.
- **Metacognition, calibration, and abstention:** deepen Claim Ledgers,
  Verification Bandwidth, World Models, Deliberation, and Readiness.
- **Reasoning-trace faithfulness:** join White-Box Evidence, Adversarial
  Evaluation, Artifact Graphs, and Policy Optimization.
- **Model welfare and moral patienthood:** retain as a constitutional and moral-
  uncertainty watchlist until operational predicates and evidence improve.
- **Domain applications:** biology, cyber, education, medicine, and finance are
  threshold and transfer domains, not automatic chapters.

Do not add separate chapters for inference serving, semantic memory, abstract
compute governance, legal compliance, model welfare, embedded agency, or every
architecture family now.
Fast Generation already owns serving-policy tradeoffs; the memory owners can
absorb semantic-memory work; Candidate L is about physical infrastructure, not
a generic compute-policy survey; Candidate J is about institutional control,
not legal advice; and the remaining items lack a distinct control plane after
the routes above. Persuasion is Candidate I under the explicit source,
boundary, competence, safety, and reader-value gates above. Embedded agency is
a named foundations gap, but it does not yet have an operational owner or an
honest empirical program sufficient for chapter admission.

## Admission and execution order

1. Finish the four already manifest-admitted chapter packets and reconcile the
   current 59-chapter truth.
2. Source-note and boundary-audit Privacy and Governed Model Training first;
   they repair owner names already assumed elsewhere in the manifest.
3. Adjudicate Objective Formation beside those repairs because Policy
   Optimization, Planning, and RSI already assume a legitimate objective they
   do not create. Reject it if the target/proxy/consumer binding and objective-
   version lifecycle cannot remain distinct from Human Intent, Constitutional
   Alignment, Moral Uncertainty, and Policy Optimization.
4. Adjudicate Physical Compute after Governed Training and Resource Economics
   freeze their abstract budget/run boundary; reject it if the physical
   capacity, environmental, community, and retirement lifecycle can be carried
   without overloading those owners.
5. Admit Perception before finalizing World Models, then Embodied Agency after
   Perception and Runtime Adapter handoffs are stable.
6. Source-note and boundary-audit Human–AI Communication after the Human
   Factors interfaces are stable. Admit it only if the evidence-bounded
   communication packet, audience/amplification authority, observed-effect
   ledger, and correction/retraction lifecycle remain a distinct control plane
   under ethical, privacy-preserving, non-manipulative evaluation.
7. Adjudicate Human–AI Organizations before Multi-Agent Dynamics; it must own
   the designed meso-level institution without absorbing single-job execution,
   one-operator control, pairwise protocol validity, or ecosystem dynamics.
8. Adjudicate Institutions and AI Deployment Transition after the organizational
   owner is stable and before the population-level chapter closes. Institutions
   must remain public-authority/cross-jurisdiction infrastructure rather than a
   law survey; AI Deployment Transition must preserve realized outcomes and
   affected denominators rather than forecasting ASI-era macroeconomics from
   exposure.
9. Admit Multi-Agent Dynamics after its empirical population program and
   gradual-disempowerment indicators are frozen.
10. Admit Autonomous Replication after the safe synthetic provider boundary and
   non-proliferation review are frozen.
11. Decide Scientific Discovery last through the general-control-plane gate.
12. Re-run chapter overlap, interface closure, reader flow, source crosswalk,
   proof targets, claim atoms, public truth, and render validation only after
   each coherent insertion—not through thirteen empty stubs.

Each candidate requires passage-reviewed primary comparators covering
mechanism/capability, limitation/failure, competing design, and

mappings; an adjacent-owner table; a complete chapter packet; a
claim-commensurate evidence plan with competence and positive controls where

to preserve the old outline.

## Non-claims

that any proposed mechanism works, that cited results reproduce locally, that
the ASI Stack is complete or state of the art, or that the book has built or
made ASI safe. It establishes a bounded, falsifiable queue for closing ownership
gaps without declaring completeness from an outdated map.
