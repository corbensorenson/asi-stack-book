# P6.4-A1 adjudication: governed model training

Date: 2026-07-19
Candidate: `governed-model-training-distributed-optimization-and-scaling`
Decision: **admit at argument support**
Manifest effect: one Part III chapter after Replaceable Cognitive Substrates
Empirical/support/release effect: none

## Decision

The candidate survives the source, boundary, competence, and reader-value
gates. It owns one previously unowned transaction: the transition from frozen
architecture, data lease, objective, optimizer, numerical policy, topology, and
budget through distributed execution, full-state checkpoint and recovery,
checkpoint-family selection, and an evidence-bounded qualification handoff.

The chapter does not own architecture choice, data rights, feedback validity,
supply-chain provenance, resource allocation, artifact custody, benchmark
qualification, readiness, or deployment. Those neighbors retain their existing
contracts. The new owner answers a narrower question none of them answers:
**is the selected checkpoint the product of the training run claimed?**

## Four-role source gate

| Required role | Admitted primary or official sources | What the role contributes | Ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mechanism or capability | Llama 3; Megatron distributed training | Concrete composed parallelism, topology/numerical policy, checkpoint and interruption case; tensor/pipeline/data interactions and optimizer semantics. | Provider and paper results are not locally reproduced or universal. |
| Limitation or failure | Llama 3; DataStates-LLM; PyTorch Distributed Checkpoint | Normal interruption burden, distributed checkpoint consistency, asynchronous completion, canonical state, strict load, and known interface preconditions. | No source proves complete application state or exact resume. |
| Competing design | ZeRO; GSPMD | Progressive state sharding versus compiler-mediated general sharding and topology composition. | Neither is accepted as universally optimal; matched comparison remains required. |
| Measurement or evaluation | MLPerf Training v6.0 | Fixed quality targets, repetitions, system metadata, variance, divisions, corrections, and time-to-quality. | Benchmark discipline is not full run integrity, safety, or release evidence. |

All seven source notes contain bounded mechanism, evidence, failure, use, and
open-question fields. The Llama 3 note was upgraded from abstract-only to a
paper-body review of Sections 3.3.1--3.3.4 and Tables 4--5.

The pinned `corbens_trainer_project` source is a separate local implementation
and failure comparator. Its typed campaigns, run-fact records, learning-truth
gates, checkpoint lineage, quarantine history, null seed/code identities,
stale promotion state, and unacknowledged asynchronous checkpoint paths sharpen
the competence burden. They do not count as an eighth external source and do
not establish a natural distributed-training or model-quality result.

## Exclusive-owner test

| Neighbor | Why returning the candidate there fails |
|---|---|
| Replaceable Cognitive Substrates | Owns architecture admission and migration, not faithful execution of one selected architecture's training process. |
| Data Engines | Owns datum and descendant rights, not distributed update order, topology, numerical semantics, or checkpoint selection. |
| Policy Optimization | Owns feedback-governed behavior updates and causal policy evidence, not base-run and distributed trainer integrity. |
| AI Supply Chain | Owns static lineage and build provenance, not dynamic batch, gradient, fault, recovery, and selection receipts. |
| Resource Economics | Owns allocation and total cost, not whether allocated resources executed the intended optimization. |
| Weight Custody | Owns holder, use, transfer, derivative, and release authority after artifacts exist, not the creation transaction and resume equivalence. |

The boundary is distinct and teachable. Removing it leaves topology drift,
numerical drift, torn checkpoints, state amnesia, data replay/skip, failed-run
censorship, and checkpoint-selection leakage without one reader owner.

## Competence burden

The authored schema, fixture, validator, and Lean module are development
controls only. They may establish record consequences, not training outcomes.
The claim-bearing campaign must:

1. use an open natural workload that both single-process and distributed strong
   baselines can competently train to a frozen target;
2. compare uninterrupted, standard distributed, weight-only recovery,
   full-state governed, and strong alternative-topology arms under matched
   information, budget, and tuning opportunity;
3. inject worker, collective, checkpoint, data-order, RNG, scheduler, scaler,
   precision, topology, stale-update, and selection-leakage faults at more than
   one timing;
4. retain every attempt, failed run, configuration search, candidate, best, and
   final checkpoint;
5. use at least three seeds and a prospectively declared equivalence class;
6. keep qualification unopened until validation selection freezes and use an
   independently implemented evaluator;
7. measure time-to-quality, silent-drift detection, resume distance, downstream
   qualification, resources, failures, operator work, and governance cost
   together; and
8. invalidate rather than interpret a campaign with an incompetent baseline,
   broken fault injector, uncertain state inventory, leaked qualification, or
   lost denominator.

This burden directly addresses the project's false-negative concern: a cheap
toy implementation or weak test cannot support rejection of the architecture.

## Reader value

The chapter adds three concepts readers cannot recover reliably from neighboring
chapters:

- training-run identity is larger than code, data, and weights;
- checkpoint authority advances through request, staging, durable commit,
  validation, resume qualification, and independent model qualification; and
- a checkpoint family and failed-run denominator are evidence objects, not
  disposable training exhaust.

It also links replaceable substrates to readiness without making Transformer
state the universal schema. Each substrate must declare its own mutable state
and equivalence conditions.

## Nonclaims and terminal disposition

Admission changes the book's argument structure only. It establishes no model
training, distributed efficiency, optimizer superiority, exact resume, fault
tolerance, model quality, safety, alignment, support transition, release,
publication, transfer, SOTA, AGI, or ASI claim.

P6.4-A1 is terminal when the manifest, chapter, seven external sources and
notes, pinned local implementation comparator, source
matrix, glossary/outline/handoffs, authored record contract, formal targets,
prospective campaign, reader audit, validators, generated surfaces, render, and
clean-main attestation agree. Empirical work remains owned by the frozen
campaign and does not reopen the structural decision.
