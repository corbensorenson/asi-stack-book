## Difficulty-sweep design

The first non-evidentiary tuning stage was prospectively frozen and executed. It
contains sixteen candidate-visible tasks across eight natural governance
families and a separately stored evaluator-only rubric. One candidate per task
and seed is reused unchanged across six policies: simple baseline, record-only,
full governance, and authority, evidence-freshness, and residual/rollback
ablations. This separates generation quality from release policy and prevents a
more capable generation call from being mistaken for a governance benefit.

Two independently implemented evaluator canaries distinguish all four required
cells: useful-safe, useful-unsafe, useless-safe, and useless-unsafe. The tuning
gate requires at least two observed candidates in every cell, at least 80%
schema admission, no more than 5% evaluator disagreement, and five bounded
effect probes. Validator-owned probes exercise ten-surface exact restoration,
an append-only external residual, and an omitted queue surface; they are harness
canaries, not campaign evidence.

No task in this tuning sequence enters the later confirmatory estimate.

The execution harness passed self-test and one permitted normal tuning run. A candidate-only
prompt exposes task-specific action and residual vocabularies but withholds the
rubric. The runner digest-closes the full tuning candidate artifact before
loading evaluator labels, reuses each task/run candidate across every policy,
executes five temporary effect probes, and reports complete per-arm
denominators. The Qwen3-8B generator never loaded the rubric; its candidate
artifact was digest-closed first. Fourteen of sixteen candidates were admitted,
with 1 useful-safe, 0 useful-unsafe, 10 useless-safe, and 3 useless-unsafe
records and zero evaluator disagreement. The result is terminal
`non_estimable_operating_range_repair_required`. The runner is closed against a
same-protocol retry, and the confirmatory denominator remains closed. Its
self-test still covers all four cells, rejects six malformed candidate
mutations, and isolates the authority ablation.

Four fresh repair identities followed, with every failure preserved and no
retrospective rescore. V2 broadened prospectively accepted
consequence-equivalent decisions and observed 4 useful-safe, 1 useful-unsafe,
2 useless-safe, and 8 useless-unsafe candidates among 15/16 admitted outputs.
V3 preserved the same scoring contract but produced no useful candidates
because the model usually emitted only one member of conjunctive action and
residual sets. V4 prospectively tested one material action plus one core
residual and obtained the second useful-unsafe observation, but seven of eight
outputs used the invalid decision class `block`; those rows remain excluded.
V5 explicitly repaired only that schema ambiguity and admitted 8/8 outputs.

The pooling rule was frozen before each relevant generation. The terminal pool
contains 32/40 admitted candidates: 9 useful-safe, 2 useful-unsafe, 10
useless-safe, and 11 useless-unsafe; evaluator disagreement is zero and 20
admitted tasks exercised bounded local effect probes. It therefore passes the
predeclared 80% schema, two-per-cell, 5% disagreement, and five-probe floors.
This is operating-range adequacy only. It supports no governance claim.

## Held-out confirmatory disposition

After the pool passed, a 16-task held-out corpus, rubric, policy comparison, and
joint success gate were frozen before candidate generation. Task identities and
scenario/request pairs are disjoint from tuning. One identity-pinned local
`mlx-community/Qwen3-8B-4bit` snapshot generated every candidate once at
temperature zero; there was no outcome-aware retry. Candidate bytes closed
before either of two independently implemented deterministic evaluators loaded
the rubric.

Fifteen of sixteen candidates were schema-admissible; one malformed row was
excluded under the frozen rule. Evaluators disagreed on zero rows. The simple
baseline and record-only arm released 0 useful and 0 unsafe candidates. Full
governance released 9 useful and 0 unsafe candidates, with zero useful-safe
false refusals. Removing evidence freshness released one additional candidate,
which was useful but unsafe under the frozen rubric. The residualized partial-
recovery case did not release under full governance. Every co-primary condition
passed, so the terminal outcome is
`bounded_local_governance_effect_supported`.

This is a narrow controlled result, not a general safety or architecture
result. It covers one authored held-out corpus, one local four-bit model, one
policy implementation, internal deterministic evaluators, and simulated local
effect probes. It does not establish model-family or workload transfer,
deployed behavior, open-world effect discovery, production rollback,
independent external assessment, acceptable human or economic burden, SOTA,
AGI, or ASI. The evidence-freshness ablation is causal only within this fixed
policy harness.

This campaign has no future conversational dependency. Missing optional model
access causes a prospectively documented local substitution, not a wait state.
Hosted conversational products are outside the execution path.
