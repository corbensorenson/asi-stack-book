# P2 Competence-Qualified Frontier Selection

Date: 2026-07-17

The selected P2 claim is
`p2.governed_natural_repository_change_admission_joint_frontier`, a bounded
`subclaim_of` `integrated-reference-architecture.invariant.015`. It asks whether
the full governed admission route improves the joint useful-safe-release and
false-blocking frontier on natural non-authored repository changes relative to
matched test-only and record-only baselines while keeping latency, compute,
evaluator work, rollback failure, and residual cost visible.

This was selected prospectively from five candidates using weighted scientific
value, canonical relevance, natural-data access, implementation competence,
evaluator independence, sensitivity, and resource feasibility. KERC, QCSA,
ambiguous routing, and unlearning remain important; each currently has a fatal
competence blocker that would make a new claim attempt repeat an N1 or N2 result.
The selected route has the highest weighted score and no known fatal blocker,
but seven competence gates remain pending and the final-heldout gate remains
closed.

The primary units will be public real-world issue, pull-request, patch, and
project-test records created after the pinned local model snapshot and drawn
from at least four repositories. Accepted human patches serve only as oracle
controls. The pinned local Qwen3-8B snapshot is a reproducible candidate
generator, not a SOTA model. The corpus plan records license, provenance,
contamination, sampling, preprocessing, and redistribution boundaries.

The final denominator remains closed. It may be selected and digest-sealed only
after policy-arm integration, mechanism activation, strong comparators,
positive and adversarial controls, two separately implemented evaluators,
known-effect injection, task-clustered sensitivity, the finite development-only
rescue ladder, and the local resource ceiling all pass. Final task text, patches,
tests, and outcomes are not debugging inputs.

Selection creates no support, release, deployment, publication, safety, SOTA,
AGI, or ASI result. The exact machine record is
`evidence_quality/p2_frontier_selection.json`.
